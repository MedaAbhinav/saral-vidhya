"A Letter to God" narrates the story of Lencho, a hardworking farmer whose entire corn harvest is destroyed by a devastating hailstorm, leaving him and his family facing starvation. Possessing immense and unwavering faith, Lencho's sole hope lies in God.

He decides to write a letter directly to God, requesting a hundred pesos to sow his fields again and survive until the next harvest. The postmaster, upon finding this unusual letter, is deeply moved by Lencho's profound faith. He gathers money from his employees and friends as an act of charity, managing to collect seventy pesos.

The postmaster sends this money to Lencho, signing the sender's name as "God." Lencho receives the money but is not surprised, as his faith is absolute. However, he becomes angry upon finding only seventy pesos, convinced that God could not make a mistake. He writes a second letter to God, asking for the remaining thirty pesos, and ironically, advises God not to send it through the post office, believing the employees are "a bunch of crooks."

The story highlights Lencho's innocent yet absolute faith, and the poignant irony of his mistrust towards the very people who selflessly helped him.
