# NCERT Class 10 English – "A Letter to God" – Study Guide

This guide is designed for standard-level CBSE board preparation, focusing on key aspects of the chapter "A Letter to God".

---

## 1. Chapter Overview

"A Letter to God" is a simple yet profound story about a farmer named Lencho, who has unwavering faith in God. When a devastating hailstorm destroys his entire corn crop, leaving him and his family without food for the year, Lencho, out of pure conviction, writes a letter to God asking for a hundred pesos to sow his field again and feed his family.

The story unfolds with a humorous and heartwarming turn when a postman discovers the letter addressed to God. The postmaster, deeply moved by Lencho's faith, decides to answer the letter himself. He collects money from his employees, contributes a part of his own salary, and manages to gather a little more than half of the requested amount (70 pesos). He sends the money to Lencho, signing the accompanying letter simply as "God."

The story culminates in a powerful display of irony. Lencho, upon receiving the money, is not surprised by God's response but becomes angry when he counts only 70 pesos. Believing God could not have made a mistake, he writes another letter, expressing his disappointment and asking God to send the remaining money directly to him, as he suspects the post office employees are a "bunch of crooks."

**Central Theme:** The story beautifully contrasts Lencho's innocent and absolute faith in God with the kindness and compassion shown by fellow human beings (the postmaster and his employees), culminating in a poignant display of dramatic and situational irony.

## 2. Key Concepts (with explanations)

1.  **Unwavering Faith:** Lencho's absolute and unquestioning belief in God's existence and benevolence. Despite his dire situation, he never doubts that God will help him, going so far as to initiate direct correspondence. His faith is the driving force of the entire narrative.
    *   *Example:* "But in the hearts of all who lived in that solitary house...there was a single hope: help from God."

2.  **Humanity and Charity:** The story highlights the innate goodness and compassion present in people. The postmaster, a stranger to Lencho, is deeply moved by his faith and selflessly decides to help him, gathering money from his colleagues and contributing his own salary, all for an "act of charity."
    *   *Example:* "...he asked for money from his employees, he himself gave part of his salary, and several friends of his were obliged to give something ‘for an act of charity’."

3.  **Irony:** This is a crucial concept in the story.
    *   **Situational Irony:** The stark contrast between what is expected and what actually happens. Lencho, who believes in God and receives help from humans (post office employees), ends up accusing those very helpers of being "crooks." The people who acted as God's agents are perceived as villains.
    *   **Dramatic Irony:** The audience (readers) knows more than Lencho. We know that the postmaster and his staff sent the money, but Lencho remains ignorant, believing it came from God directly and that the post office staff stole part of it. This creates a sense of poignancy and mild humour.

4.  **Nature's Dual Role:** Nature is depicted as both a provider and a destroyer. The rain initially promises a good harvest, bringing joy to Lencho, but then turns into a destructive hailstorm, devastating his livelihood.
    *   *Example:* "The only thing the earth needed was a downpour or at least a shower... But suddenly a strong wind began to blow and along with the rain very large hailstones began to fall."

5.  **Optimism vs. Reality:** Lencho's initial optimism about the rain (likening raindrops to 'new coins') quickly turns into despair and sorrow when the hailstones destroy his entire crop, highlighting the unpredictability of life and nature.

## 3. Definitions and Terminology

*   **Crest:** The top of a hill or mountain.
*   **Dotted:** Scattered with, marked here and there with.
*   **Downpour:** A very heavy fall of rain.
*   **Intimately:** In a close, private, or personal manner; thoroughly.
*   **Draped:** Covered or decorated with cloth or something similar.
*   **Hailstones:** Pellets of ice falling during a hailstorm.
*   **Frozen pearls:** A metaphor used to describe hailstones, emphasizing their beauty and destructive nature.
*   **Locusts:** Swarms of destructive flying insects that attack and destroy crops.
*   **Conscience:** An inner sense of what is right or wrong that guides a person's behaviour.
*   **Peso:** The basic monetary unit of several Latin American countries.
*   **Amiable:** Having or displaying a friendly and pleasant manner.
*   **Resolution:** A firm decision to do or not to do something.
*   **Obliged:** Under an obligation; required to do something.
*   **Contentment:** A state of happiness and satisfaction.
*   **Wrinkling of his brow:** An expression caused by effort, concentration, or worry.
*   **Affixed:** Stick, attach, or fasten (something) to something else.
*   **Bunch of crooks:** A group of dishonest people or swindlers (used by Lencho to describe the post office employees).

## 4. Formulas / Rules / Diagrams to Know (Literary Devices & Writing Structure)

For an English chapter, this section focuses on literary analysis tools and understanding textual structures.

1.  **Literary Devices:**
    *   **Metaphor:** A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.
        *   *Examples:* "They are new coins. The big drops are ten cent pieces and the little ones are fives." (Raindrops as coins), "Frozen pearls" (Hailstones as pearls).
    *   **Simile:** A figure of speech involving the comparison of one thing with another thing of a different kind, used to make a description more emphatic or vivid (using 'like' or 'as').
        *   *Example:* "The field was white, as if covered with salt."
    *   **Personification:** The attribution of a personal nature or human characteristics to something non-human, or the representation of an abstract quality in human form.
        *   *Example:* "The earth needed a downpour." (Earth needing something, as if it has desires)
    *   **Irony:** (As explained in Key Concepts) The expression of one's meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite, typically for humorous or emphatic effect. This is a central device in the story.
    *   **Allusion:** An indirect or passing reference to an event, person, place, or artistic work. Lencho's absolute faith can be seen as an allusion to traditional religious belief.

2.  **Character Sketch Structure:** When asked to describe a character (e.g., Lencho, Postmaster), follow a logical structure:
    *   **Introduction:** Briefly introduce the character.
    *   **Key Traits:** List 2-3 main characteristics (e.g., Lencho: Pious, hardworking, innocent, simple-minded, optimistic, easily angered). Provide evidence from the text for each.
    *   **Role in the Story:** How the character contributes to the plot and themes.
    *   **Conclusion:** Summarize their significance.

3.  **Theme Analysis Structure:** When asked to discuss a theme:
    *   **Introduction:** Define the theme and state its relevance to the story.
    *   **Elaboration with Examples:** Explain how the theme is developed through plot points, character actions, and dialogue. Provide specific textual examples.
    *   **Impact/Significance:** Discuss why this theme is important and what message it conveys.
    *   **Conclusion:** Summarize the theme's overall contribution.

4.  **Descriptive Language:** Notice how authors use adjectives, adverbs, and sensory details to create vivid imagery and atmosphere (e.g., "field of ripe corn dotted with the flowers," "huge mountains of clouds," "air was fresh and sweet").

## 5. Common Mistakes Students Make

1.  **Misunderstanding Irony:** Many students fail to grasp the irony, especially the situational irony where Lencho's trust in God leads him to accuse the very people who helped him.
2.  **Confusing Faith with Foolishness:** While Lencho's faith is extreme, it's important to present it as unwavering and pure, rather than simply foolish or naive, as it's the core of his character and the story's message.
3.  **Ignoring the Postmaster's Role:** Overlooking the postmaster's significant act of kindness and empathy. His character is crucial for highlighting humanity and charity.
4.  **Simplistic Answers:** Providing brief, one-sentence answers without elaboration or textual evidence, especially for analytical questions.
5.  **Lack of Vocabulary:** Not using appropriate literary terms or descriptive language from the chapter.
6.  **Spelling and Grammatical Errors:** These can reduce marks, especially in English exams. Proofreading is essential.
7.  **Not Adhering to Word Limits:** Writing too little or too much, which can affect time management and the quality of answers.
8.  **Misinterpreting Questions:** Not reading the question carefully and answering something slightly different from what was asked.

## 6. Exam Tips (CBSE-specific)

1.  **Read Questions Carefully:** Understand what each question is asking. Identify keywords like "describe," "explain," "analyze," "contrast," "compare."
2.  **Structure Your Answers:**
    *   **Short Answer Type (2-3 marks):** Direct and concise answers. State your point clearly and provide brief explanation or textual reference.
    *   **Long Answer Type (5-6 marks):**
        *   **Introduction:** Briefly introduce the main idea.
        *   **Body Paragraphs:** Break down your answer into 2-3 distinct points. Each paragraph should focus on one idea, supported by examples from the text.
        *   **Conclusion:** Summarize your points and provide a final thought.
3.  **Use Textual Evidence:** Support your points with direct quotes or clear references to events and dialogues from the story. This adds credibility to your answers.
4.  **Focus on Themes and Characterization:** Be prepared to discuss the main themes (faith, humanity, irony) and provide character sketches for Lencho and the Postmaster.
5.  **Practice Value-Based Questions:** These questions often ask you to connect the story's message to real-life values or ethical dilemmas. For example, "What qualities of the postmaster would you like to imbibe?"
6.  **Enhance Vocabulary:** Use relevant vocabulary from the chapter and general English terms to articulate your ideas effectively.
7.  **Time Management:** Allocate time for each section and question. Don't spend too much time on one question, especially in the short answer section.
8.  **Revision:** Always revise your answers for grammatical errors, spelling mistakes, and coherence. Ensure your points are clear and well-expressed.
9.  **Understand Literary Devices:** Be ready to identify and explain the use of metaphors, similes, and especially irony in the story.

## 7. Quick Revision Points

*   **Lencho:** Farmer, extreme faith in God, hardworking, innocent, simple-minded.
*   **Problem:** Hailstorm destroys ripe corn crop completely.
*   **Solution (Lencho's):** Writes a letter to God, asking for 100 pesos.
*   **Letter's Destination:** Post office, found by a postman.
*   **Postmaster's Reaction:** Amused initially, then deeply moved by Lencho's faith.
*   **Postmaster's Action:** Decides to answer the letter to preserve Lencho's faith.
*   **Collection:** Gathers money (part of his salary, from employees, friends) for "an act of charity."
*   **Amount Collected:** 70 pesos (couldn't gather 100).
*   **Delivery:** Sent the money in an envelope, signed "God."
*   **Lencho's Reaction (1st letter):** No surprise, but anger upon counting only 70 pesos.
*   **Lencho's Belief:** God cannot make a mistake; post office employees stole the rest.
*   **Lencho's Second Letter:** Asks God for the remaining money, but not through the post office, calling the employees a "bunch of crooks."
*   **Key Themes:** Unwavering faith, human charity/kindness, irony (situational and dramatic), nature's dual role.
*   **Literary Devices:** Metaphor (raindrops as coins, hailstones as frozen pearls), Simile (field white as if covered with salt), Irony (central to the plot).
