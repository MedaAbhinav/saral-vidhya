### 2. Short Answer Questions (2–3 marks)

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions in 30-40 words.

1.  Why did Lencho have high hopes for a good harvest that year?
    *   **Model Answer:** Lencho was a dedicated farmer who knew his fields intimately. His cornfield was ripe and dotted with flowers, promising a good harvest. The only thing it needed was a downpour or at least a shower, which he was confidently expecting after observing the sky.

2.  What did Lencho mean by calling the raindrops "new coins"?
    *   **Model Answer:** Lencho called the raindrops "new coins" because they were crucial for a good harvest. A successful crop would bring him prosperity and financial gain, much like money. The big drops were 'ten cent pieces' and the little ones 'fives', signifying their value.

3.  How did the hailstones affect Lencho's field and his family's prospects?
    *   **Model Answer:** The hailstones completely destroyed Lencho's cornfield. Not a single leaf remained on the trees, and the flowers were gone from the plants. This meant no corn for the year, plunging Lencho and his family into the prospect of hunger and sorrow.

4.  What was Lencho's only hope after the devastating hailstorm? How did he decide to act upon it?
    *   **Model Answer:** Lencho's only hope after the hailstorm was "help from God." Driven by his immense faith, he decided to write a letter directly to God, explaining his plight and requesting a hundred pesos to sow his field again and support his family.

5.  Describe the postmaster's initial reaction when he saw Lencho's letter to God.
    *   **Model Answer:** The postmaster, a fat and amiable fellow, first laughed heartily upon seeing a letter addressed to God. He found it amusing and unusual, as he had never encountered such an address in his career.

6.  Why did the postmaster decide to answer Lencho's letter?
    *   **Model Answer:** The postmaster was deeply moved by Lencho's unwavering faith in God. He didn't want to shake such profound faith and decided to answer the letter to uphold it. He felt it was his responsibility to act as an agent of God.

7.  How did the postmaster and his employees contribute to help Lencho?
    *   **Model Answer:** The postmaster requested money from his employees, gave a part of his own salary, and persuaded several friends to contribute for an "act of charity." Their collective effort was aimed at providing financial assistance to Lencho.

8.  Why did Lencho show "not the slightest surprise" on seeing the money in the envelope?
    *   **Model Answer:** Lencho showed no surprise because his faith in God was absolute and unquestioning. He was completely confident that God would respond to his request and send him the money, so receiving it was merely an expected outcome for him.

9.  Why did Lencho become angry when he counted the money?
    *   **Model Answer:** Lencho became angry because he found only seventy pesos, not the hundred pesos he had requested. He believed that God could not have made a mistake, nor would He have denied him what he had asked for, leading him to suspect the post office employees.

10. What did Lencho ask for in his second letter to God?
    *   **Model Answer:** In his second letter, Lencho asked God to send him the remaining thirty pesos, as he desperately needed them. He also specifically requested that the money should not be sent through the mail, implying his distrust of the post office employees, whom he called "a bunch of crooks."

### 3. Long Answer Questions (5 marks)

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions in 100-120 words.

1.  Lencho's faith in God is the central theme of the story. Discuss how his faith is portrayed and how it influences his actions throughout the narrative.
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Lencho's unwavering and absolute faith in God despite extreme adversity.
        *   **Initial Actions:** Believes God will provide a good harvest (hopes for rain).
        *   **After Hailstorm:** Even after total destruction, his 'one hope' is God. No human can help, only God.
        *   **Writing the Letter:** His faith is so strong that he believes God will read and respond to a direct letter, asking for a specific amount. He knows how to write, but attributes knowledge of all to God.
        *   **Receiving Money:** No surprise, just expectation. He believes God sent the money directly.
        *   **Second Letter:** Blames humans (post office) for the discrepancy, not God. His faith in God remains intact, believing God cannot err.
        *   **Conclusion:** His faith is pure, simple, and unshakeable, forming the backbone of his character and driving every decision.

2.  "What faith! I wish I had the faith of the man who wrote this letter." Elaborate on the postmaster's statement and explain how he actively worked to preserve Lencho's faith.
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Postmaster's Reaction:** Initially amused, but quickly turns serious, deeply impressed by the depth of Lencho's faith. He admires such unwavering belief.
        *   **His Resolution:** Decides not to let Lencho's faith be shaken. Feels a moral obligation to maintain it.
        *   **Collective Effort:** Initiates a collection from his employees, contributes a significant part of his own salary, and persuades friends to donate for "an act of charity."
        *   **The Letter:** Collects 'a little more than half' the amount. Puts the money in an envelope and signs it simply as "God" to keep the illusion alive.
        *   **Motivation:** His actions stem from empathy, respect for faith, and a desire to do a good deed, experiencing "contentment" in the process.
        *   **Conclusion:** The postmaster acts as an earthly representative of God, demonstrating profound human kindness to protect a simple man's extraordinary faith.

3.  The story "A Letter to God" uses irony effectively. Discuss the various instances of irony in the story, especially focusing on the climax.
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Irony is a contrast between expectation and reality. The story is rich in situational irony.
        *   **Initial Hope vs. Reality:** Lencho hopes for rain for a good harvest, but the rain turns into a destructive hailstorm.
        *   **Lencho's Faith vs. Post Office's Role:** Lencho believes only God can help him, but it is human generosity (the post office employees) that actually provides the aid.
        *   **Climax - The Second Letter:** This is the strongest irony. The very people who sacrificed to help Lencho are accused by him of being "a bunch of crooks."
        *   **Expected Gratitude vs. Actual Anger:** The postmaster expects Lencho to be happy or grateful, but Lencho is angry because he received less than he expected, suspecting his helpers.
        *   **Conclusion:** The irony highlights Lencho's naive yet profound faith, his inability to see beyond his literal interpretation, and the bitter truth that good deeds can sometimes be misunderstood or unappreciated.

4.  Despite his anger, Lencho is not a villain. Discuss his character, highlighting his simplicity, hard work, and unshakeable faith.
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Lencho, though seemingly ungrateful, is presented as a protagonist whose core qualities are admirable.
        *   **Hard-working Farmer:** An "ox of a man," works tirelessly in his fields, intimately familiar with his land. His livelihood depends entirely on agriculture.
        *   **Simple and Naive:** His worldview is straightforward – God is a tangible helper. He doesn't question the logistics of writing to God or receiving money.
        *   **Unshakeable Faith:** This is his defining trait. He has absolute confidence in God's existence and benevolence. Even after receiving less money, he blames humans, not God.
        *   **Paternal Responsibility:** His concern is for his family's survival, prompting his desperate plea to God.
        *   **Anger Explained:** His anger stems from his unblemished faith (God cannot make a mistake) and his immediate need, not malice. He truly believes the post office intervened wrongly.
        *   **Conclusion:** Lencho embodies the simple, devout, and hardworking common man, whose faith, though seemingly misplaced in human terms, provides him strength in adversity.

5.  How does the story "A Letter to God" portray the human spirit's response to adversity, both from Lencho's perspective and that of the post office employees?
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** The story showcases two distinct yet powerful responses to adversity: resilience through faith (Lencho) and compassion through action (post office).
        *   **Lencho's Response:**
            *   **Initial Despair:** "All our work, for nothing... We'll all go hungry this year."
            *   **Resilience through Faith:** Quickly shifts from despair to unwavering hope in God.
            *   **Proactive Action:** Writes a letter, despite his limited literacy, demonstrating his determination to overcome the crisis. His faith provides strength and a concrete path forward.
        *   **Post Office Employees' Response:**
            *   **Empathy and Kindness:** The postmaster, initially amused, is deeply moved by Lencho's faith and plight.
            *   **Collective Action:** Employees contribute money, showing a collective human spirit of generosity and charity. They understand Lencho's desperation.
            *   **Protecting Faith:** Their goal is not just to provide money but to preserve a fellow human's innocent faith, which is a powerful act of kindness.
        *   **Conclusion:** The story beautifully contrasts Lencho's simple, almost primal faith-driven survival with the sophisticated, empathetic, and philanthropic response of the post office, both demonstrating admirable aspects of the human spirit in the face of hardship.

### 4. HOTS (Higher Order Thinking Skills) Questions

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions creatively and critically.

1.  If Lencho had access to modern communication (e.g., email, mobile phone), how might his story have unfolded differently? Discuss the role of his socio-economic context in shaping his actions.
    *   **Guidance:** Consider if he would have tried to find government aid, contacted agricultural experts, or still turned to 'God' via technology. Discuss how his rural, isolated, and simple background made direct mail to God a plausible solution for him. Would he be more 'aware' of intermediaries if he had broader access to information?

2.  The story ends with Lencho blaming the post office employees, calling them "a bunch of crooks." Do you think Lencho was justified in his anger, or was he ungrateful? Present an argument for both sides.
    *   **Guidance:**
        *   **Justified (from Lencho's perspective):** His absolute faith told him God would send exactly 100 pesos. The discrepancy could only be human interference. He was desperate.
        *   **Ungrateful:** He failed to acknowledge the immense kindness and sacrifice of the post office employees. He didn't consider that 'God' often works through human instruments. His faith was pure but also narrow-minded in its literal interpretation.
        *   **Conclusion:** Acknowledge the complexity; it's a conflict between absolute faith and human reality.

3.  "Faith can move mountains." Discuss how this proverb relates to Lencho's story, considering both the literal and metaphorical meanings.
    *   **Guidance:**
        *   **Literal (from Lencho's perspective):** Lencho's faith is so strong that he literally believes God will intervene directly to solve his problem (sending money), a 'mountain' of financial crisis.
        *   **Metaphorical (from the story's overall message):** Lencho's faith, though directed at God, metaphorically "moves" the postmaster and his employees (humans) to act. It inspires them to overcome their own constraints and collect money, effectively moving the 'mountain' of Lencho's despair and providing him with hope.
        *   **Conclusion:** The proverb is ironically fulfilled through human kindness, demonstrating that faith, even if naive, can evoke powerful responses.
