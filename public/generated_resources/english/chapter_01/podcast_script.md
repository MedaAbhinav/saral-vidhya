## Podcast Script: The English Lit Lounge - "A Letter to God"

**Episode Title:** Unshakeable Faith: Exploring "A Letter to God"

**Total Time:** 10 minutes

---

**(Intro Music Fades In and Out)**

**Riya (Host):** Hello, young learners, and welcome back to The English Lit Lounge! I’m your host, Riya, and today we’re diving into a powerful and thought-provoking story from your NCERT Class 10 English textbook, "First Flight." It's a tale that explores the very essence of faith, hope, and the human spirit: "A Letter to God."

And to help us navigate the fascinating themes and details of this chapter, we have an expert with us today – a beloved English teacher with years of experience, Ms. Sharma. Welcome to the show, Ma’am!

**Expert Teacher:** Thank you, Riya! It’s a pleasure to be here and talk about one of my favourite stories.

**Riya:** Ma'am, "A Letter to God" immediately grabs your attention with its title. The NCERT textbook begins by saying, "They say faith can move mountains. But what should we put our faith in? This is the question this story delicately poses." Can you set the stage for us – who is Lencho, and what's his predicament?

**Expert Teacher:** Absolutely, Riya. Lencho is our protagonist, a simple, hardworking farmer living in a solitary house on a low hill. His entire livelihood depends on his fields of ripe corn, which, at the beginning of our story, are "dotted with flowers that always promised a good harvest." He's intimately connected to his land, and his immediate hope is for "a downpour or at least a shower" to ensure a successful crop. He’s a man who understands nature and relies on it for his survival.

**Riya:** So, a farmer waiting for rain – a common sight in many parts of the world. What happens next? Does his wish come true?

**Expert Teacher:** Initially, yes! Lencho, observing the sky, predicts rain, and soon enough, big drops begin to fall. He's ecstatic! He compares the raindrops to "new coins" – the big drops are ten-cent pieces, and the little ones are fives. This shows how crucial the rain is for his financial well-being; he sees it as wealth. He even goes out just to feel the rain on his body, a moment of pure joy and gratitude.

**Riya:** That sounds wonderful, but I sense a twist coming. Nature can be fickle, can't it?

**Expert Teacher:** Precisely. Just as Lencho is admiring his fields, "draped in a curtain of rain," the weather takes a turn for the worse. A strong wind begins to blow, and along with the rain, "very large hailstones" start to fall. The boys run out to collect these "frozen pearls," but Lencho knows this is a disaster. He hopes it passes quickly, but it doesn't. For a whole hour, the hail devastates everything – the house, the garden, the hillside, the cornfield, the entire valley. The field turns white, "as if covered with salt."

**Riya:** Oh no, that’s heartbreaking! So, all his hopes, all his hard work, destroyed in an hour? What’s his reaction to this devastation?

**Expert Teacher:** His soul is "filled with sadness." He stands in the middle of his ruined field and utters a profound statement to his sons: "A plague of locusts would have left more than this. The hail has left nothing. This year we will have no corn." This highlights the sheer scale of the destruction. It's a night of sorrow for them, thinking, "All our work, for nothing." and "We’ll all go hungry this year."

**Riya:** That's truly a desperate situation. But amidst this despair, the chapter talks about a "single hope: help from God." This brings us to the core concept of the story, doesn't it, Ma'am?

**Expert Teacher:** Indeed, Riya. This is where Lencho's unwavering faith truly shines. Despite the total loss, they remind themselves, "no one dies of hunger." And for Lencho, this translates into an absolute, unquestioning belief in God's providence. He thinks only of "the help of God, whose eyes... see everything, even what is deep in one’s conscience." He's described as "an ox of a man," meaning incredibly hardworking, but also simple in his faith. For him, God is a tangible helper, and he decides to write a letter to God, asking for a hundred pesos. This is the central act that sets the rest of the story in motion. His faith is so pure and strong that he believes this is a viable solution.

**Riya:** So, the key takeaway from the initial part of the story is Lencho’s profound faith, even in the face of absolute ruin. It’s a powerful lesson in hope and belief. Thank you, Ma'am, for breaking that down for us. Now, for our listeners, let's test your understanding with a quick quiz based on what we've just discussed!

---

**(Short transition sound effect)**

**Riya:** Alright, everyone, get ready. Here are three questions for you:

1.  **Question 1:** What was the "only thing the earth needed" according to Lencho before the actual rainfall?
    *(Pause for a few seconds)*
    **Answer:** A downpour or at least a shower.

2.  **Question 2:** Why did Lencho say the big drops of rain were like "ten cent pieces" and the little ones like "fives"?
    *(Pause for a few seconds)*
    **Answer:** Because the rain was essential for his harvest, and he viewed it as a source of wealth or money.

3.  **Question 3:** What natural phenomenon ultimately destroyed Lencho’s cornfield and filled his soul with sadness?
    *(Pause for a few seconds)*
    **Answer:** A hailstorm.

---

**Riya:** How did you do? I hope you got them all right! Ms. Sharma, any final thoughts on the beginning of this remarkable story?

**Expert Teacher:** Just that "A Letter to God" is more than just a story; it’s a reflection on what we hold dearest and where we turn in times of crisis. Lencho’s unwavering faith, even when everything seems lost, is a beautiful and inspiring example. It makes you think about your own beliefs and where you find hope. I encourage everyone to read the full story to see how Lencho's letter is received and what happens next!

**Riya:** Excellent point, Ma’am. This story certainly leaves us pondering the nature of faith and resilience. Thank you so much, Ms. Sharma, for joining us and illuminating the initial chapters of "A Letter to God."

**Expert Teacher:** My pleasure, Riya.

**Riya:** And thank YOU, dear listeners, for tuning into The English Lit Lounge. We hope this discussion has given you a deeper appreciation for Lencho’s story and inspired you to explore the chapter further. Don't forget to read the entire story in your NCERT Class 10 English "First Flight" textbook. Join us next time for more literary adventures!
