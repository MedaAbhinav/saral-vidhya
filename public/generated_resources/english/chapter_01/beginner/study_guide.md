## "A Letter to God" - Easy Study Guide

### 1. What is this chapter about?
This chapter tells the story of Lencho, a simple farmer who lives on a hilltop. When a big hailstorm ruins all his crops, he has nowhere else to turn. He decides to write a letter asking God for money, showing his incredibly strong faith, and the surprising things that happen next.

### 2. Key Terms (Simple Definitions & Examples!)
*   **Faith:** Believing in something very strongly, even if you can't see it or prove it.
    *   *Example:* Like how you have faith that the sun will rise every morning.
*   **Hailstorm:** A heavy rain where small pieces of ice (like tiny frozen balls) fall from the sky instead of just water.
    *   *Example:* Imagine it raining tiny, hard ice cubes – that's a hailstorm!
*   **Pesos:** A type of money used in some countries.
    *   *Example:* Just like we use 'Rupees' in India, Lencho needed 'Pesos'.
*   **Postmaster:** The main person or boss who runs a post office.
    *   *Example:* He's like the principal of your school, but for the post office.
*   **Charity:** Giving help or money to people who are in need, just because you want to be kind.
    *   *Example:* Donating old clothes or toys to children who don't have many.

### 3. Main Points to Remember
*   Lencho was a hardworking farmer whose only source of income was his cornfield.
*   One day, heavy rain came, which was good, but it quickly turned into a **hailstorm** that completely destroyed all his crops.
*   Lencho had **unshakeable faith** in God. He believed God would surely help him in his time of need.
*   He decided to write a **letter directly to God**, asking for 100 pesos to plant new crops and feed his family.
*   A **postman** found the letter addressed "To God" and showed it to the **postmaster**.
*   The postmaster, a kind and friendly man, was very impressed by Lencho's strong faith. He didn't want Lencho's faith to be broken.
*   So, the postmaster, his employees, and some friends collected money out of **charity** to help Lencho. They managed to collect a little more than half (70 pesos).
*   They put the money in an envelope and signed it "God," so Lencho would think God had sent it.
*   Lencho was happy to receive a letter from God, but he got **angry** when he counted the money and found only 70 pesos.
*   He believed God could not make a mistake, so he thought the **post office employees had stolen** the missing 30 pesos.
*   He wrote another letter to God, asking Him to send the rest of the money, but told God **not to send it through the post office** because he thought the employees were "a bunch of crooks."

### 4. Simple Examples (Things you can compare to!)
*   **Lencho's Faith:** Imagine you're completely lost, but you truly believe your parents will find you, no matter what. That's how much Lencho believed in God!
*   **Destroyed Crops:** Think about working very hard on a beautiful drawing for a competition, and suddenly, someone accidentally tears it right before you have to submit it. Lencho felt that kind of crushing sadness.
*   **Helping in Secret:** If you secretly help a friend with their homework when they're struggling, without letting them know it was you, it's a bit like how the postmaster helped Lencho.
*   **Misunderstanding:** Imagine you get a surprise gift from a friend, but you mistakenly think they gave you the wrong thing on purpose. Lencho thought the kind helpers were actually thieves!

### 5. Quick Revision Checklist
Before your exam, try to tick these off!
*   Do I know who Lencho is and what he wanted?
*   Can I explain what destroyed Lencho's crops?
*   Do I understand *why* Lencho wrote a letter to God?
*   Do I know who actually read Lencho's letter and helped him?
*   Can I explain Lencho's reaction when he received the money?
*   Do I remember what Lencho wrote in his *second* letter?
*   Can I tell someone the main message of the story (about faith and human kindness)?

### 6. Remember This!
*   **Lencho's faith in God was incredibly strong and unwavering, even in the face of disaster.**
*   **The postmaster and his staff showed great human kindness and generosity by helping a stranger.**
*   **The story has a touch of irony: the very people who helped Lencho were called "a bunch of crooks" by him because of his misunderstanding.**
