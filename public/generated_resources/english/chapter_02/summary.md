The chapter "Nelson Mandela: Long Walk to Freedom" chronicles the historic inauguration of Nelson Mandela as South Africa's first Black President on May 10, 1994. This event marked the definitive end of **apartheid**, a brutal system of racial segregation and white supremacy that had oppressed the black population for centuries.

After decades of struggle and 30 years in prison, Mandela's African National Congress won the first democratic, non-racial elections. The inauguration ceremony, held in Pretoria, was a momentous "rainbow gathering" of international dignitaries, signifying a global victory for **justice, peace, and human dignity**. Mandela, in his address, profoundly pledged to **liberate all South Africans from poverty, deprivation, suffering, gender, and other forms of discrimination**. He solemnly vowed that "never, never again" would the beautiful land experience the oppression of one by another, envisioning a new society founded on **equality, freedom, and human pride**. This moment celebrated political emancipation and a glorious step towards a truly democratic nation.
