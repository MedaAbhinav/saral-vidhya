# Course Offering: The Sermon at Benares – A Journey Through Grief and Wisdom

---

## Course Title
**The Sermon at Benares: A Journey Through Grief and Wisdom**

## Course Description
This course offers an in-depth exploration of "The Sermon at Benares," a poignant prose piece from the NCERT Class 10 English curriculum. Students will delve into the profound story of Kisa Gotami and her encounter with the Buddha, examining themes of grief, loss, impermanence, and the universal nature of suffering. Through close reading, critical analysis, and philosophical discussion, participants will not only enhance their linguistic and analytical skills but also gain valuable insights into the wisdom encapsulated in this timeless narrative. This course is designed to foster a deeper appreciation for the text, prepare students for board examinations, and encourage thoughtful reflection on life's fundamental truths.

## Learning Objectives
Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
1.  **Summarize** the plot and **identify** the key characters and their motivations in "The Sermon at Benares."
2.  **Articulate** and **analyze** the central themes of the story, including grief, impermanence, suffering, and the path to acceptance.
3.  **Identify** and **explain** the literary devices employed in the text, such as symbolism, narrative technique, and character development.
4.  **Understand** and **apply** new vocabulary and expressions encountered in the text to improve their overall English language proficiency.
5.  **Critically reflect** on the universal message of the Buddha's sermon and **connect** it to personal experiences or broader philosophical concepts.
6.  **Express** their interpretations and analyses of the text clearly and coherently in both written and oral forms, adhering to academic standards.

## Target Audience
NCERT Class 10 students preparing for their Board Examinations, educators seeking comprehensive teaching resources, and anyone interested in exploring moral and philosophical narratives through English literature.

## Prerequisites
*   Basic proficiency in English reading comprehension and writing at a Class 9 level.
*   Access to the NCERT Class 10 English textbook ("First Flight").
*   A willingness to engage in critical thinking, discussion, and reflective activities.

## Module Breakdown

### Module 1: Introduction to the Text and Context (Approx. 2 hours)
*   **Topic 1.1:** Overview of "The Sermon at Benares" – First impressions and initial reading.
*   **Topic 1.2:** Authorial Background and Context – Buddhist Jataka Tales, the life and teachings of Gautama Buddha.
*   **Topic 1.3:** Setting the Scene – Ancient India, Benares, and the cultural landscape.
*   **Topic 1.4:** Pre-reading Vocabulary – Key terms and phrases for enhanced comprehension.

### Module 2: Kisa Gotami's Grief and Quest (Approx. 3 hours)
*   **Topic 2.1:** Close Reading: Part 1 – Kisa Gotami's profound sorrow and desperate search for a cure for her dead child.
*   **Topic 2.2:** Character Analysis: Kisa Gotami – Her journey from denial to understanding.
*   **Topic 2.3:** Exploring Themes: The nature of grief, maternal love, and the initial human reaction to loss.
*   **Topic 2.4:** Literary Focus: Narrative structure and the beginning of Kisa's transformation.

### Module 3: The Buddha's Wisdom and the Sermon (Approx. 3 hours)
*   **Topic 3.1:** Close Reading: Part 2 – Kisa's encounter with the Buddha and his unconventional "sermon."
*   **Topic 3.2:** Character Analysis: Gautama Buddha – His compassion and profound teaching methodology.
*   **Topic 3.3:** The "Sermon": The task of the mustard seeds and its symbolic significance.
*   **Topic 3.4:** Exploring Themes: Impermanence (Anicca), suffering (Dukkha), and the universality of death.

### Module 4: Universal Truths and Philosophical Reflection (Approx. 2 hours)
*   **Topic 4.1:** Kisa Gotami's Realization – The turning point and her acceptance of the truth.
*   **Topic 4.2:** Connecting to Life – How the sermon applies to universal human experience and suffering.
*   **Topic 4.3:** Moral and Ethical Lessons – The importance of acceptance, letting go, and finding peace.
*   **Topic 4.4:** Discussion and Debate – Relating the text to modern contexts and personal insights.

### Module 5: Language, Literary Devices & Exam Preparation (Approx. 2 hours)
*   **Topic 5.1:** Advanced Vocabulary and Figurative Language – Identifying and understanding metaphors, similes, and other literary devices.
*   **Topic 5.2:** Textual Analysis Skills – Extract-based questions, inferential comprehension.
*   **Topic 5.3:** Essay Writing Strategies – Crafting analytical essays on themes and character.
*   **Topic 5.4:** Comprehensive Review – Recapitulation of key concepts and practice questions for board examinations.

## Assessment Plan
*   **Formative Assessments (Continuous):**
    *   Weekly short quizzes (vocabulary, comprehension checks).
    *   Active participation in class discussions and group activities.
    *   Short answer questions and paragraph responses based on specific textual extracts.
    *   Mini-presentations or role-playing key scenes.
*   **Summative Assessments (End-of-Course):**
    *   **Analytical Essay (50%):** A 300-400 word essay analyzing a major theme (e.g., "The journey from grief to enlightenment") or character (e.g., "Kisa Gotami's transformation").
    *   **Textual Analysis Test (30%):** A short test comprising multiple-choice questions, extract-based comprehension questions, and short answer questions covering all modules.
    *   **Vocabulary & Grammar Application (20%):** A test focused on the specific vocabulary introduced in the course and its correct usage in sentences.

## Duration
Approximately 10-12 hours of guided instruction, typically spread over 2-3 weeks, depending on student pace and depth of discussion.

## Difficulty Level
**Moderate.** While the language is accessible for Class 10 students, the course encourages analytical thinking, critical reflection on complex philosophical themes, and advanced literary interpretation, moving beyond mere comprehension.
