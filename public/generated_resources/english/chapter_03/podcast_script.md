**Riya:** Hello, high-flyers, and welcome back to "Lit-Up Learners," your go-to podcast for dissecting the fascinating world of NCERT English! I'm your host, Riya, and today, we're taking off into the exciting realm of ambition, fear, and courage with Chapter 3 from your Class 10 textbook: "Two Stories about Flying."

Joining us today to navigate these tales is our expert literary guide, Ms. Sharma. Welcome, Ms. Sharma!

**Ms. Sharma:** Thank you, Riya! It's a pleasure to be here and delve into these inspiring stories with our young listeners.

**Riya:** Absolutely! "Two Stories about Flying" is a unique chapter because it brings together two distinct narratives: "His First Flight" by Liam O'Flaherty and "The Black Aeroplane" by Frederick Forsyth. Both touch on the dream of conquering the skies, but in very different ways. Ms. Sharma, let's start with the first story, "His First Flight." It's a truly relatable tale, isn't it?

**Ms. Sharma:** It certainly is, Riya. "His First Flight" is a powerful allegory for overcoming fear and stepping out of your comfort zone. The story introduces us to a young seagull, terrified of flying, even though his siblings have already mastered it. He feels certain his wings won't support him.

**Riya:** That fear is so palpable in the text. His parents try everything – calling to him shrilly, upbraiding him, even threatening to let him starve on his ledge. Talk about tough love!

**Ms. Sharma:** Indeed. The parents represent the push we sometimes need from our support systems. They understand that the seagull will never learn to fly if he stays on the ledge. They demonstrate different teaching methods: encouragement for his siblings, and then more drastic measures for him. The mother, in particular, plays a crucial role.

**Riya:** Ah, yes, the mother's strategy! She sees his hunger, which has been building for twenty-four hours, and uses it. She tears a piece of fish and flies towards him, but then halts, just out of reach. That must have been maddening for the hungry seagull!

**(Sound of a seagull cry, faint)**

**Ms. Sharma:** Precisely! It's a brilliant psychological tactic. Maddened by hunger, the young seagull dives at the fish. For a moment, he's seized by monstrous terror, falling outwards and downwards. His heart stands still. But in that critical moment, his instincts kick in. His wings spread out, the wind rushes against them, and he finds himself soaring.

**Riya:** And just like that, the fear vanishes! He's not falling headlong; he's flying! And he completely forgets he was ever afraid. It's such a beautiful moment of triumph. What are the key takeaways from this story, Ms. Sharma?

**Ms. Sharma:** The most significant themes are **overcoming fear**, **self-belief**, and **the importance of perseverance**. The young seagull's story teaches us that often, the biggest barrier to achieving our goals is our own apprehension. Sometimes, we need a push, a little "tough love," or even a desperate situation to unlock our true potential. It's about trusting your instincts and taking that leap of faith.

**Riya:** A fantastic message! Now, let's briefly touch upon the second story, "The Black Aeroplane." This one takes a very different turn, venturing into mystery and the unknown.

**Ms. Sharma:** Yes, "The Black Aeroplane" is a thrilling and mysterious tale. It follows a pilot flying his old Dakota aeroplane back to England. He dreams of his holiday and a hearty breakfast. Suddenly, he encounters a massive, dark storm cloud. All his instruments fail, and he's lost in the terrifying darkness.

**(Sound of wind howling, distant thunder)**

**Riya:** A truly terrifying situation for any pilot! What happens next?

**Ms. Sharma:** Just when he feels all hope is lost, he spots another aeroplane, a mysterious black one, flying next to him through the storm. He follows this strange plane, which guides him safely out of the storm and to the runway. But when he lands and asks about the other pilot, no one has seen another plane in the storm.

**Riya:** Goosebumps! It leaves you wondering, doesn't it? Was it an angel, a figment of his imagination, or a guardian spirit? What's the central idea here?

**Ms. Sharma:** This story explores themes of **courage in adversity**, **faith in the unknown**, and the idea of an **unseen helping hand**. It highlights how, in moments of extreme crisis, we might find unexpected guidance or strength, sometimes from an inexplicable source. Both stories, in their own way, talk about conquering challenges in the air.

**Riya:** Fascinating! So, whether it's an internal battle against fear like the young seagull, or an external battle against a raging storm like the pilot, both stories underscore the human spirit's capacity to overcome.

**Ms. Sharma:** Exactly. They both celebrate resilience and the journey towards achieving what seems impossible.

**Riya:** Alright, it's time for our quick quiz segment! Listeners, grab a pen and paper, or just shout out your answers. Ms. Sharma, are you ready?

**Ms. Sharma:** Always ready, Riya!

**Riya:** Excellent! Here’s Question 1:

**Quiz Question 1:** What was the young seagull's main reason for not wanting to fly initially?
**(Pause for a few seconds)**
**Riya:** And the answer is… He felt certain his wings would never support him and was terrified of the great expanse of sea beneath him.

**Riya:** Moving on to Question 2:

**Quiz Question 2:** What specific action by his mother finally prompted the young seagull to make his first flight?
**(Pause for a few seconds)**
**Riya:** The answer: She tore at a piece of fish and flew across to him, halting just out of reach, maddening him with hunger.

**Riya:** And finally, Question 3, a bit more thought-provoking:

**Quiz Question 3:** What universal lesson can we draw from the young seagull's experience of his first flight?
**(Pause for a few seconds)**
**Riya:** The lesson is: Overcoming our fears and stepping out of our comfort zone is essential for growth and achieving our potential, even if it requires a leap of faith.
