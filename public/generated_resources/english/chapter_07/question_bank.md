### 2. Short Answer Questions (2-3 Marks)

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions in 30-40 words.

1.  **What was Valli's strongest desire and how did it grow?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli's strongest desire was to ride on the bus, even if just once. This wish grew stronger day by day as she watched the bus filled with new passengers, sparking longings, dreams, and hopes within her, becoming an overwhelming desire.

2.  **How did Valli gather all the details about the bus journey?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli gathered details by listening carefully to conversations between her neighbours and regular bus users. She also asked a few discreet questions here and there, slowly piecing together information about the fare, journey time, and route.

3.  **Describe the bus Valli rode on.**
    *   **Model Answer:** The bus Valli rode on was new, with its outside painted a gleaming white and green stripes along the sides. Inside, the overhead bars shone like silver, and the seats were soft and luxurious. There was also a beautiful clock above the windshield.

4.  **Why did Valli refuse the conductor's help to get on the bus?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli refused the conductor's help because she was fiercely independent and proud. She wanted to demonstrate that she was capable of getting on the bus by herself, asserting her 'grown-up' status.

5.  **Why did the conductor call Valli "Madam"?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The conductor, being a jolly and good-natured person, called Valli "Madam" as a humorous gesture. He was amused by her serious demeanour, her confident demand to ride the bus, and her insistence on being treated as an adult despite her young age.

6.  **What did Valli find 'repulsive' about the elderly woman sitting next to her?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli found the elderly woman repulsive because of her appearance and habits. She had big holes in her earlobes with ugly earrings, and she was chewing betel nut, with the juice about to dribble out of her mouth.

7.  **What made Valli overcome with shyness on the bus?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli was overcome with shyness when the other passengers on the bus started looking at her and laughing along with the conductor's jokes about her being a "madam." This unexpected attention made her self-conscious.

8.  **Why did Valli insist, "I am not a child, I tell you, I’m eight years old"?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli insisted this to an elderly man and the conductor because she wanted to be taken seriously and treated as an adult. She felt her age of eight years was significant enough to warrant adult respect and not be patronized or called a "child."

9.  **What did Valli see on her outward journey that filled her with joy?**
    *   **Model Answer:** On her outward journey, Valli saw a young cow, tail high in the air, running very fast right in the middle of the road. It was startled by the bus and kept running, which Valli found immensely funny and delightful.

10. **How did Valli react upon seeing the dead cow on her return journey?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli was deeply saddened and distressed upon seeing the dead cow. The image of the beautiful, lively cow she had seen earlier, now lying lifeless, deeply impacted her. It changed her mood from joy to melancholy and made her think about the mystery of life and death.

### 3. Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions in 100-120 words. Provide an outline answer.

1.  **Valli's bus journey can be seen as her first induction into the mystery of life and death. Discuss.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Valli's bus journey is not just a ride but a profound experience that exposes her to life's realities.
        *   **Initial Joy (Life):** Her excitement and observation of the lively world outside – the running cow, scenic beauty, new people – represent the vibrant aspect of life.
        *   **The Shock of Death:** The sight of the same cow, now lifeless and brutally killed, shatters her innocence. It's an abrupt confrontation with mortality.
        *   **Gap between Knowing and Understanding:** She knows death exists, but seeing it transforms abstract knowledge into a concrete, unsettling understanding. Her joy turns to sadness, indicating this shift.
        *   **Conclusion:** The journey marks Valli's transition from innocent childhood perceptions to a deeper, more mature (though still nascent) comprehension of the cycle of life and death, as highlighted in the story's introduction.

2.  **Describe the meticulous planning Valli undertook to fulfill her desire of riding the bus.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Valli's profound desire to ride the bus led her to meticulous and careful planning.
        *   **Information Gathering:** She discreetly listened to conversations of neighbours and regular bus passengers, asking questions here and there.
        *   **Key Details Acquired:** She learned the bus fare (30 paise one way), the distance to town (six miles), and the journey time (45 minutes).
        *   **Financial Management:** She understood the need to save money, resisting temptations like peppermints, toys, and rides on the merry-go-round at the village fair.
        *   **Timing Strategy:** She calculated the timing for the one-o’clock afternoon bus to allow her to reach town, stay on the bus, and return before her mother awoke from her nap.
        *   **Conclusion:** Her persistent curiosity, careful observation, and self-discipline allowed her to gather all necessary information and funds, demonstrating her extraordinary determination for an eight-year-old.

3.  **How does the bus conductor contribute to making Valli's first bus journey memorable and special?**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** The conductor, a jolly and amiable character, plays a significant role in Valli's memorable first bus journey.
        *   **Humorous Interaction:** He immediately engages with Valli's unusual request to stop the bus for her, playfully addressing her as "madam."
        *   **Friendly Demeanor:** His constant teasing and joking, like asking everyone to make way for "madam," ease Valli's initial shyness and make other passengers laugh.
        *   **Concern and Care:** Despite his jokes, he shows genuine concern, advising her to sit down to avoid injury, reflecting his responsible nature.
        *   **Treating her as an Equal (Ironically):** While calling her madam, he treats her with a mock-adult respect, which Valli both appreciates and sometimes resents, adding to the charm of her experience.
        *   **Conclusion:** His light-hearted nature and friendly interactions ensure that Valli's journey, despite its eventual somber turn, is initially filled with warmth and humor, making it a unique and special memory for the young girl.

4.  **Valli was a highly observant and perceptive child. Justify this statement with examples from the story.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Valli's character is defined by her keen observational skills and perceptive understanding of her surroundings.
        *   **Bus Details:** She meticulously observes the bus route, timings, fare, and even the types of passengers, all from her doorway vantage point.
        *   **Journey Observations:** On the bus, she 'devours everything with her eyes,' noticing the gleaming white exterior, green stripes, silver bars, soft seats, and the clock.
        *   **Nature's Beauty:** Her descriptions of the canal, palm trees, mountains, green fields, and the running cow indicate her appreciation for details and beauty.
        *   **People's Behavior:** She notices the "repulsive" details of the elderly woman and the concern of the elderly man, showing her judgment of character.
        *   **Understanding Life and Death:** Her profound reaction to the dead cow demonstrates her ability to not just see, but to grasp the deeper implications of life and mortality.
        *   **Conclusion:** Valli’s journey is enriched by her acute observations, making her not just a participant but a mindful observer who gains significant insights into the world.

5.  **What does Valli's decision not to get off the bus at the town tell you about her character and priorities?**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Valli's choice to stay on the bus in town reveals her practical mindset, underlying caution, and strict adherence to her original plan.
        *   **Financial Prudence:** She had saved precisely sixty paise for the round trip. Getting off and buying a drink (even if cheap) would deplete her funds, preventing her return.
        *   **Adherence to Plan:** Her primary goal was to *ride the bus*, not necessarily to explore the town. Deviating from her plan was not an option for her.
        *   **Underlying Childish Fear:** Despite her bravado, there's a hint of childish fear of venturing into an unknown place alone, reinforced by the warnings she might have heard.
        *   **Sense of Responsibility:** She understood the importance of returning home before her mother awoke, showing a sense of responsibility to maintain secrecy.
        *   **Conclusion:** This decision highlights her strong will, her meticulous planning, her ability to prioritize her goals (the ride itself), and a subtle acknowledgment of her own limitations, blending her mature planning with her youthful cautiousness.

### 4. HOTS (Higher Order Thinking Skills) Questions

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions thoughtfully, requiring analysis and critical thinking.

1.  **The story states that Valli's journey was her "induction into the mystery of life and death" and that "she sees the gap between our knowing that there is death, and our understanding of it." How does Valli's experience with the cow specifically illustrate this profound statement?**
    *   **Answer Guidance:** Students should explain that "knowing" death is an intellectual concept (everyone knows people/animals die). "Understanding" it, however, involves emotional processing and experiencing its finality. Valli's initial joy at seeing the lively cow represents life. The shock of seeing the *same* cow, now dead, motionless, and ugly, transforms her abstract knowledge of death into a visceral, emotional understanding. The contrast between its vibrant past and its desolate present reveals the abrupt, irreversible nature of death, causing a shift from simple happiness to a mature sense of melancholy and contemplation. This personal experience bridges the gap between mere information and genuine comprehension.

2.  **Despite her strong desire to act and be treated like an adult, Valli displays several moments of childishness throughout her journey. Discuss the paradox in Valli's character, providing examples from the text.**
    *   **Answer Guidance:** This question requires analyzing Valli's conflicting traits. Students should acknowledge her adult-like qualities: meticulous planning, financial discipline, independence (refusing help, paying her own fare), and proud assertion of her age. However, they must also point out her childish aspects: her shyness when passengers laugh at her, her haughty "I'm not a child!" outbursts, her repulsion towards the elderly woman's appearance (a common childish reaction to perceived ugliness), her ultimate fear preventing her from stepping off the bus into the town, and her simple joy at the running cow. The paradox lies in her attempting to control her world like an adult while still possessing the emotional reactions and vulnerabilities of an eight-year-old.

3.  **Valli meticulously plans her bus journey, saving money and gathering information discreetly. What does this tell us about the nature of dreams and aspirations, especially for someone in Valli's circumstances, and the efforts required to achieve them?**
    *   **Answer Guidance:** This question asks for an interpretation of Valli's actions in a broader context. Students should discuss how Valli's planning emphasizes that even seemingly simple dreams require significant effort and sacrifice, especially when resources are limited (like 30 paise being "a fortune" to her). Her discreet information gathering shows resourcefulness and determination. It highlights that true aspiration often involves patience, self-discipline (resisting temptations), and a strategic approach. For Valli, her circumstances as a young girl in a village meant this journey was a huge undertaking, transforming a simple wish into a project of meticulous execution. It teaches that fulfilling one's dreams, regardless of scale, often depends on personal initiative, perseverance, and careful preparation.
