### II. Short Answer Questions (2-3 Marks)

**Directions:** Answer the following questions in 30-40 words.

1.  How did Valli gather detailed information about the bus journey?
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli gathered information by listening carefully to conversations between her neighbours and people who regularly used the bus. She also asked a few discreet questions here and there to fill in the gaps in her knowledge about the route, fare, and duration.

2.  Describe the conductor's attitude towards Valli during her bus journey.
    *   **Model Answer:** The conductor was a jolly, good-natured, and helpful person, fond of joking. He treated Valli with playful respect, calling her "madam" and teasing her good-humouredly, while also showing genuine concern for her safety.

3.  What specific sights did Valli enjoy observing outside the bus on her way to town?
    *   **Model Answer:** On her way to town, Valli was thrilled to see a canal, swaying palm trees, vast grasslands, distant mountains, a deep ditch, and acres of lush green fields. A highlight was a young cow running enthusiastically in the middle of the road.

4.  Why did Valli stand up on her seat during a part of her journey?
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli stood up on her seat because a canvas blind covered the lower part of her window, obstructing her view of the outside world. To fully enjoy and devour the beautiful scenery, she peered over the blind.

5.  What was Valli's reaction when the conductor called her 'madam' for the first time?
    *   **Model Answer:** Initially, Valli was annoyed and shot an angry glance at the conductor, asserting that she was not a madam. However, as everyone laughed and the conductor continued his playful banter, she gradually joined in the laughter, accepting the humour.

6.  How did Valli manage to save enough money for her bus fare?
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli managed to save money for her bus fare by resisting various temptations. She had to suppress her desire for peppermints, toys, balloons, and going on the merry-go-round at the village fair, meticulously saving every stray coin to accumulate the sixty paise needed for the round trip.

7.  What kind of bus did Valli ride in, and what were its key features?
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli rode in a new bus, which was painted a gleaming white with some green stripes along the sides. Inside, the overhead bars shone like silver, and the seats were soft and luxurious, with a beautiful clock above the windshield.

8.  Why was Valli initially overcome with shyness when she boarded the bus?
    *   **Model Answer:** Valli was overcome with shyness because it was her first time travelling alone, and all the existing passengers on the bus (about six or seven) were looking at her and laughing along with the conductor's jokes about her being a "madam."

9.  What was the "source of unending joy" for Valli?
    *   **Model Answer:** The bus that travelled between her village and the nearest town was a source of unending joy for Valli. The sight of it, filled each time with a new set of passengers, fascinated her and fueled her desire to ride it.

10. How did the elderly man's concern affect Valli during the journey?
    *   **Model Answer:** An elderly man, genuinely concerned, told Valli to sit down as she was standing. However, Valli, proud of her independence and having paid her fare, was annoyed by his attention and responded haughtily, asserting her right to stand.

### III. Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

**Directions:** Answer the following questions in 100-120 words.

1.  Describe Valli's first bus journey to town. What aspects made it a truly exciting and memorable experience for her?
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Preparation:** Mention her meticulous planning, saving money, and gathering information.
        *   **Boarding:** Highlight her independence in refusing help and paying her fare, her interaction with the jolly conductor who called her 'madam', and her initial shyness.
        *   **Outward Journey:** Describe her excitement in observing the new, gleaming bus interior and the picturesque scenery outside (canal, fields, mountains, and especially the playful cow). Mention her assertion of self against the elderly man and woman.
        *   **Excitement Factors:** The novelty of the journey, the feeling of independence, the beautiful sights, and the fulfilment of her long-cherished dream all contributed to making it an incredibly exciting and memorable experience.

2.  The bus journey served as Valli's initiation into the mysteries of life and death. Discuss how this journey transformed her understanding.
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Innocent Beginning:** Valli starts her journey with childlike joy and wonder, captivated by the world outside her village and the vibrant life around her (e.g., the lively young cow).
        *   **The Turning Point:** The crucial incident of seeing the same cow, now lifeless and brutally dead, on her return journey.
        *   **Realization:** This stark contrast between life and death forces Valli to confront the fragile and transient nature of existence. Her initial joy is replaced by a profound sense of sadness and introspection.
        *   **Transformation:** The journey changes her from a purely joyous child to a more thoughtful and mature individual who has glimpsed the harsh realities and inevitable end of life. She returns home with a deeper, more solemn understanding, going beyond mere 'knowing' to truly 'understanding' death.

3.  The conductor plays a significant role in making Valli's first bus journey special. Comment on his character traits and how he interacts with Valli.
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Character Traits:** The conductor is portrayed as jolly, good-humoured, helpful, empathetic, and playfully respectful. He is observant of Valli's independent spirit.
        *   **Playful Interactions:** He engages in light-hearted banter with Valli, calling her "madam," which initially annoys her but later makes her laugh. He respects her insistence on doing things herself, despite offering help.
        *   **Concern for Safety:** He shows genuine concern by advising her to sit down to avoid falling when the bus might make a sharp turn.
        *   **Empathy and Understanding:** On the return journey, after Valli sees the dead cow, he notices her sadness and offers a gentle, philosophical remark, showing sensitivity to her changed mood.
        *   **Overall Impact:** His friendly and understanding nature makes Valli's journey not just a ride but a comfortable and memorable experience, contributing to her overall learning.

4.  Valli was a meticulous planner for her bus journey. Elaborate on the steps she took to prepare for her adventure.
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Motivation:** Her overwhelming desire to ride the bus, even if just once, drove her to meticulous planning.
        *   **Information Gathering:** She carefully listened to conversations between neighbours and bus regulars. She also asked discreet questions to learn details like the distance to town (six miles), the one-way fare (thirty paise), and the journey duration (forty-five minutes).
        *   **Financial Planning:** Valli understood she needed sixty paise for a round trip. She saved every stray coin, resisting strong temptations like buying peppermints, toys, balloons, or riding the merry-go-round at the village fair.
        *   **Time Management:** She calculated the total journey time (one hour and thirty minutes) and identified the one-o’clock afternoon bus as ideal, allowing her to be back home by about two forty-five, before her mother awoke from her afternoon nap.
        *   **Demonstration of Qualities:** Her planning showcased her remarkable determination, resourcefulness, and maturity beyond her eight years.

5.  What qualities of Valli are revealed through her actions and interactions during her bus journey?
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Curiosity & Observant:** Her initial fascination with the bus, her keen observation of the passengers and scenery (bus interior, canal, fields, cow).
        *   **Determination & Strong Will:** Her unwavering desire to ride the bus, followed by her meticulous planning and saving, demonstrates a strong will to achieve her goal.
        *   **Independence & Self-Reliance:** She insists on paying her own fare, refuses the conductor's help to get on, and proudly asserts her age, showing a desire to be treated as an adult.
        *   **Pride & Sensitivity:** Her annoyance when called a 'child' or when elderly passengers offer unwanted advice/attention highlights her pride and sensitivity about her age and capabilities.
        *   **Maturity (developed):** While initially childish in some ways, her profound sadness and reflection after witnessing the dead cow reveal a significant step towards maturity and a deeper understanding of life's realities.

### IV. Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS) Questions

**Directions:** Answer the following questions in 80-100 words. These questions require critical thinking and deeper analysis.

1.  The story ends with Valli re-entering her domestic world but with a newfound understanding. Discuss how her bus journey acts as a metaphor for her initiation into the complexities of life.
    *   **Outline Answer:** Valli's bus journey transcends a mere trip; it metaphorically represents her 'first flight' into the world beyond childhood innocence. The outward journey, filled with wonder and independence, symbolizes the joys, adventures, and new experiences life offers. Her interactions with the conductor and other passengers expose her to different social dynamics. The pivotal moment is encountering the dead cow on the return. This stark image introduces her to the inevitable reality of death and loss, which is a fundamental 'mystery' of life. She returns home quieter, no longer just curious, but introspective, having been initiated into the bittersweet complexities of existence, bridging the gap between simply 'knowing' about life and death and truly 'understanding' it.

2.  If Valli had not encountered the dead cow, how might her understanding of the world have been different upon returning home? What impact did this specific incident have on her maturity?
    *   **Outline Answer:** Had Valli not encountered the dead cow, her bus journey would have likely remained a purely joyous and triumphant adventure. She would have returned home proud of her independence and exhilarated by the sights, without any profound introspection. Her understanding of the world would have been limited to its exciting and pleasant aspects. The dead cow, however, served as a crucial catalyst for her maturity. This specific incident shattered her childish bliss, abruptly confronting her with the harsh reality of mortality and the transient nature of life. It forced her to grapple with a deep philosophical concept, transforming her excitement into a reflective sadness, thereby accelerating her emotional and intellectual growth beyond her eight years.

3.  Do you think Valli was truly 'madam' as the conductor playfully called her, or was it just an ironic title? Justify your answer with examples from the text.
    *   **Outline Answer:** The conductor's initial calling Valli 'madam' was indeed ironic, highlighting the humour in an eight-year-old demanding to ride a bus alone. He was teasing her small stature versus her commanding behaviour. However, as the journey progresses, the title gains a subtle layer of truth. Valli displays many 'madam-like' qualities: her meticulous planning, her determination to save money, her assertion of independence by refusing help, her pride in paying her own fare, and her critical observations of others (like the elderly woman). By the end, her profound reflection after seeing the dead cow reveals a maturity and gravitas that transcends her age, suggesting she embodied a certain 'madam-like' wisdom. Thus, it evolves from a purely ironic title to one that partly describes her evolving persona.
