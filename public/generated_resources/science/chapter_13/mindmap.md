  root(Our Environment)

      Definition(All interacting organisms & non-living constituents)
      Components
        Biotic(Living Organisms)
          Producers(Autotrophs)
            Examples(Green plants, certain bacteria)
            Function(Make food by photosynthesis using solar energy)
          Consumers(Heterotrophs)
            Dependence(On producers directly/indirectly)
            Types
              Herbivores(Eat producers)
              Carnivores(Eat other consumers)
              Omnivores(Eat producers & consumers)
              Parasites(Feed on other living organisms)
          Decomposers
            Examples(Bacteria, fungi)
            Function(Break down dead remains & waste into simple inorganic substances)
            Importance(Natural replenishment of soil)
        Abiotic(Non-living constituents)
          Examples(Temperature, rainfall, wind, soil, minerals)
      Types
        Natural Ecosystems
          Examples(Forests, ponds, lakes)
        Human-made (Artificial) Ecosystems
          Examples(Gardens, crop-fields, aquarium)
    Food Chains and Webs
      Food Chain
        Definition(Series of organisms feeding on one another)
        Trophic Levels
          Level 1(Producers)
          Level 2(Primary Consumers - Herbivores)
          Level 3(Secondary Consumers - Carnivores/Omnivores)
          Level 4(Tertiary Consumers - Top Carnivores)
      Food Web
        Definition(Interconnected food chains)
        Importance(More stable ecosystem)
    Human Impact on Environment
      Pollution(Mentioned implicitly through "environment is not what it used to be")
      Resource depletion(Implied by "global summits")
