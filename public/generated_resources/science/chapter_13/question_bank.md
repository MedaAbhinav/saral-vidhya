### 2. Short Answer Questions (2-3 Marks) - 10 Questions

1.  **Define Ecosystem and its components.**
    *   **Model Answer:** An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (biotic components) interacting with each other and their physical environment (abiotic components) in a particular area. The components include biotic factors like producers, consumers, and decomposers, and abiotic factors like temperature, rainfall, soil, and minerals.

2.  **What are producers? Give two examples.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Producers are organisms that produce their own food, usually through photosynthesis, using inorganic substances and solar energy. They form the first trophic level. Examples: Green plants (like trees, grasses) and certain bacteria.

3.  **Differentiate between herbivores, carnivores, and omnivores.**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   **Herbivores:** Organisms that feed only on plants. (e.g., Deer, cow)
        *   **Carnivores:** Organisms that feed on other animals. (e.g., Lion, tiger)
        *   **Omnivores:** Organisms that feed on both plants and animals. (e.g., Humans, bears)

4.  **Explain the role of decomposers in an ecosystem.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Decomposers, like bacteria and fungi, break down dead organisms and waste products into simpler inorganic substances. This process recycles nutrients back into the soil, making them available for producers and maintaining the balance of the ecosystem.

5.  **What is a food chain? Illustrate with an example.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A food chain represents a linear sequence of organisms where nutrients and energy are transferred from one trophic level to another as one organism eats another. Example: Grass (producer) → Grasshopper (primary consumer) → Frog (secondary consumer) → Snake (tertiary consumer).

6.  **What is a food web? How does it differ from a food chain?**
    *   **Model Answer:** A food web consists of multiple interconnected food chains. It represents the complex feeding relationships in an ecosystem, where an organism may be prey for several different predators and may also prey on several different organisms. It is more realistic than a simple food chain.

7.  **Explain the significance of the 10% energy transfer rule in a food chain.**
    *   **Model Answer:** The 10% energy transfer rule states that only about 10% of the energy from one trophic level is transferred to the next. The remaining 90% is lost as heat, used for metabolic processes, or goes undigested. This limits the number of trophic levels in a food chain.

8.  **Why is the flow of energy in an ecosystem unidirectional?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The flow of energy is unidirectional because energy is captured by producers from sunlight and then moves sequentially up through different trophic levels. At each level, a significant portion of energy is lost as heat and is not available to be transferred back to the previous level or to the sun.

9.  **Give two examples of natural ecosystems and two examples of artificial ecosystems.**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   **Natural Ecosystems:** Forest, pond.
        *   **Artificial Ecosystems:** Aquarium, crop field.

10. **What are trophic levels? Name the organisms found at the first and second trophic levels.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Trophic levels are the stages in a food chain where energy is transferred from one organism to another. The first trophic level consists of producers (e.g., green plants). The second trophic level consists of primary consumers or herbivores (e.g., grasshoppers, deer).

### 3. Long Answer Questions (5 Marks) - 5 Questions

1.  **Explain the concept of an ecosystem. Describe its biotic and abiotic components with examples.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   Define ecosystem: Interaction between living organisms and their physical surroundings.
        *   Biotic Components: Living organisms.
            *   Producers: Make own food (photosynthesis) - green plants, algae.
            *   Consumers: Obtain food by eating others - herbivores, carnivores, omnivores, parasites.
            *   Decomposers: Break down dead organic matter - bacteria, fungi.
        *   Abiotic Components: Non-living factors.
            *   Physical factors: Temperature, rainfall, wind, sunlight.
            *   Chemical factors: Soil composition, water, minerals.
        *   Interdependence: Explain how they interact and maintain balance.

2.  **Describe the formation of a food chain and a food web. Explain the energy flow through different trophic levels with the help of the 10% law.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   Food Chain: Linear sequence, transfer of energy and nutrients, steps = trophic levels.
        *   Trophic Levels: Producers (1st), Primary Consumers (2nd), Secondary Consumers (3rd), Tertiary Consumers (4th).
        *   Food Web: Interconnected food chains, complex feeding relationships.
        *   Energy Flow:
            *   Start with solar energy captured by producers.
            *   Explain the 10% law: Only 10% energy transferred to the next level.
            *   Mention energy loss at each step (heat, metabolism).
            *   Consequence: Limits the number of trophic levels.
            *   Unidirectional flow of energy.

3.  **Discuss the role of decomposers in maintaining the balance of an ecosystem. What would happen if decomposers were absent?**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   Define decomposers (bacteria, fungi).
        *   Explain their function: Breaking down dead organic matter and waste.
        *   Process: Conversion of complex organic matter to simple inorganic nutrients.
        *   Importance: Nutrient recycling, making nutrients available for producers.
        *   Scenario without decomposers:
            *   Accumulation of dead bodies and waste.
            *   Soil fertility depletion.
            *   Disruption of nutrient cycles.
            *   Ecosystem collapse.

4.  **Differentiate between natural and artificial ecosystems. Provide examples and discuss the characteristics of each.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Natural Ecosystems:**
            *   Formation: Developed naturally over time.
            *   Examples: Forest, pond, ocean.
            *   Characteristics: Self-sustaining, complex food webs, high biodiversity, minimal human intervention.
        *   **Artificial Ecosystems:**
            *   Formation: Created and managed by humans.
            *   Examples: Aquarium, crop field, garden.
            *   Characteristics: Simpler structure, lower biodiversity, require human intervention (e.g., feeding, cleaning, irrigation), less stable.
        *   Comparison: Focus on sustainability, complexity, and human role.

5.  **Explain the concept of trophic levels. Describe the types of consumers found at different trophic levels and provide examples.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   Define trophic level: Position in a food chain.
        *   First Trophic Level: Producers (autotrophs) - Green plants, algae.
        *   Second Trophic Level: Primary Consumers (herbivores) - Deer, grasshoppers, rabbits.
        *   Third Trophic Level: Secondary Consumers (carnivores or omnivores) - Snakes, frogs, foxes.
        *   Fourth Trophic Level: Tertiary Consumers (carnivores or omnivores) - Lions, eagles, owls.
        *   Explain the energy transfer between levels and the diminishing energy.

### 4. Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS) Questions - 3 Questions

1.  **Imagine a scenario where all the decomposers in a forest ecosystem suddenly disappear. Discuss the short-term and long-term consequences for the forest.**
    *   **Hint:** Consider the accumulation of dead organic matter, nutrient availability for plants, and the overall functioning of the food web.

2.  **An ecologist observes that in a particular grassland ecosystem, the population of primary consumers is very high, while the population of tertiary consumers is very low. Explain the possible reasons for this observation, considering the principles of energy flow and food chains.**
    *   **Hint:** Relate this to the 10% law of energy transfer and the pyramid of numbers/biomass.

3.  **A farmer is planning to introduce a new type of insectivorous bird into his crop field to control the pest population. Analyze the potential benefits and drawbacks of this action from an ecological perspective, considering the concept of food webs and ecosystem balance.**
    *   **Hint:** Think about how the bird might affect other organisms in the field and whether it could disrupt existing relationships.
