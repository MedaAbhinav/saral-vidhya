### 2. Short Answer Questions (SAQs) - 2-3 Marks Each

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions in 2-3 sentences or with brief explanations.

1.  **Define 'ecosystem' and list its two main components.**
    *   **Model Answer:** An ecosystem is a system formed by the interaction of all living organisms (biotic components) with the physical environment (abiotic components) in a particular area. The two main components are biotic components (living organisms like plants, animals, microorganisms) and abiotic components (non-living factors like temperature, rainfall, soil, air).

2.  **What is the role of producers in an ecosystem? Give two examples.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Producers are organisms that produce their own food, usually through photosynthesis using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide. They form the base of the food chain and make energy available to other organisms. Examples include green plants (like trees, grasses) and certain bacteria.

3.  **Explain the concept of a food chain. Give an example of a simple food chain with three trophic levels.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A food chain is a linear sequence of organisms where nutrients and energy are transferred from one organism to another as one is eaten by another. For example: Grass (Producer) → Deer (Primary Consumer/Herbivore) → Lion (Secondary Consumer/Carnivore).

4.  **Why is the energy transfer between trophic levels generally inefficient?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The energy transfer between trophic levels is inefficient because a significant portion of the energy consumed is lost as heat during metabolic processes, used for digestion, or for performing life activities. Only about 10% of the energy is incorporated into the biomass of the next trophic level.

5.  **Differentiate between a natural ecosystem and a human-made ecosystem.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A natural ecosystem is established by nature without direct human intervention (e.g., forests, ponds). A human-made ecosystem is created and managed by humans to serve specific purposes (e.g., gardens, crop fields, aquariums).

6.  **What are decomposers and what is their significance in the environment?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Decomposers are organisms, primarily bacteria and fungi, that break down complex dead organic matter and waste products into simpler inorganic substances. Their significance lies in recycling nutrients back into the soil, making them available for producers and maintaining the balance of the ecosystem.

7.  **What is a trophic level? Name the first trophic level in most ecosystems.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A trophic level represents a step or position in a food chain occupied by a group of organisms with similar feeding modes. The first trophic level in most ecosystems is occupied by the producers (autotrophs), which are typically green plants and photosynthetic bacteria.

8.  **Explain why food chains usually consist of only three or four trophic levels.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Food chains usually have only three or four trophic levels because of the significant loss of energy at each transfer. By the time energy reaches the fourth trophic level, very little usable energy remains to support further levels.

9.  **What is a food web? How does it differ from a food chain?**
    *   **Model Answer:** A food web is a complex network of interconnected food chains in an ecosystem, showing multiple feeding relationships. It differs from a food chain, which is a single, linear sequence of organisms. A food web provides a more realistic representation of energy flow as most organisms consume or are consumed by more than one type of organism.

10. **Give two examples of abiotic factors found in a terrestrial ecosystem.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Two examples of abiotic factors in a terrestrial ecosystem are temperature and sunlight. Other examples include rainfall, wind, soil composition, and atmospheric gases.

### 3. Long Answer Questions (LAQs) - 5 Marks Each

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions in detail, providing explanations and examples.

1.  **Describe the structure and functioning of an ecosystem. Explain the roles of biotic and abiotic components with examples.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Define ecosystem and state its interactive nature.
        *   **Components:**
            *   **Biotic Components:**
                *   Producers (Autotrophs): Role (food production), examples (green plants).
                *   Consumers (Heterotrophs): Role (consumption), types (herbivores, carnivores, omnivores, parasites) with examples.
                *   Decomposers: Role (nutrient recycling), examples (bacteria, fungi).
            *   **Abiotic Components:** Role (support life), examples (temperature, rainfall, sunlight, soil, air, water).
        *   **Functioning:**
            *   Energy Flow: Unidirectional, 10% law, role of sunlight.
            *   Nutrient Cycling: Importance of decomposers.
            *   Interactions: Predator-prey, symbiotic relationships.
        *   **Conclusion:** Emphasize the balance and interdependence.

2.  **Explain the concept of trophic levels. Illustrate with a food chain, detailing the energy transfer at each level.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Define trophic level and its significance.
        *   **Trophic Levels:**
            *   First Trophic Level: Producers (autotrophs) - capture solar energy.
            *   Second Trophic Level: Primary Consumers (herbivores) - feed on producers.
            *   Third Trophic Level: Secondary Consumers (carnivores/omnivores) - feed on primary consumers.
            *   Fourth Trophic Level: Tertiary Consumers (carnivores) - feed on secondary consumers.
        *   **Energy Transfer:**
            *   Explain the 10% law of energy transfer.
            *   Mention energy loss as heat, metabolic processes.
            *   Illustrate with a specific food chain (e.g., Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake) and show approximate energy at each level (e.g., 1000 J → 100 J → 10 J → 1 J).
        *   **Conclusion:** Reinforce the idea of diminishing energy and limiting the length of food chains.

3.  **What is a food web? Explain its importance and how it differs from a food chain, using examples.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Define food web as interconnected food chains.
        *   **Structure of Food Web:** Describe the complexity and branching nature.
        *   **Importance:**
            *   Stability: Provides stability to the ecosystem by offering alternative food sources.
            *   Adaptability: Allows organisms to adapt to changes in prey availability.
            *   Realism: More accurately depicts feeding relationships in nature.
        *   **Difference from Food Chain:**
            *   Food Chain: Linear, single pathway.
            *   Food Web: Interconnected, multiple pathways.
        *   **Examples:** Provide an example of how a food chain (e.g., Grass → Rabbit → Fox) can be part of a larger food web where rabbits also eat carrots, and foxes also eat mice, and mice eat seeds.

4.  **Discuss the impact of human activities on the environment, focusing on how we disrupt the balance in natural ecosystems.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Briefly mention the interconnectedness of humans and the environment.
        *   **Disruption of Ecosystems:**
            *   **Pollution:**
                *   Air pollution (e.g., industrial emissions, vehicle exhaust) affecting plant growth and animal health.
                *   Water pollution (e.g., industrial waste, sewage) harming aquatic life and contaminating water sources.
                *   Land pollution (e.g., plastic waste, agricultural chemicals) affecting soil fertility and food chains.
            *   **Deforestation:** Loss of habitat, soil erosion, disruption of water cycles, reduction in producers.
            *   **Overexploitation of Resources:** Depletion of natural resources, extinction of species.
            *   **Habitat Destruction/Fragmentation:** Impact on biodiversity and species survival.
        *   **Consequences:** Loss of biodiversity, climate change, degradation of natural systems.
        *   **Conclusion:** Emphasize the need for sustainable practices.

5.  **Explain the flow of energy through different trophic levels in an ecosystem. What are the implications of this flow for the number of organisms at each level?**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Source of Energy:** Solar energy captured by producers.
        *   **Energy Transfer:**
            *   Explain the process of energy capture by producers (photosynthesis).
            *   Describe the transfer of energy to primary consumers, secondary consumers, and tertiary consumers.
            *   Reiterate the 10% law and reasons for energy loss (heat, metabolism).
        *   **Implications for Population Size:**
            *   Explain the pyramid of numbers and biomass.
            *   The decreasing amount of energy available at higher trophic levels limits the population size.
            *   There are generally more producers than primary consumers, more primary consumers than secondary consumers, and so on.
        *   **Unidirectional Flow:** Mention that energy flows in one direction and is not recycled.
        *   **Conclusion:** Link energy flow to the structure and dynamics of ecosystems.

### 4. Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS) Questions

1.  **Imagine a scenario where all decomposers suddenly disappear from the Earth. Describe the long-term consequences for the planet's ecosystems and the survival of other organisms.**
    *   **Hint:** Think about what would happen to dead bodies, waste, nutrient cycling, and the availability of raw materials for producers.

2.  **If an ecosystem has a very long food chain (e.g., 6 trophic levels), what are the likely consequences for the top consumers in terms of energy availability and population size? Justify your answer based on the principles of energy transfer.**
    *   **Hint:** Consider the cumulative energy loss at each step of a food chain.

3.  **An artificial pond ecosystem is established with specific aquatic plants and fish. If the oxygen pump malfunctions and stops supplying oxygen, what might happen to the fish and plants in the pond over time, and why? Relate your answer to the roles of different organisms in an ecosystem.**
    *   **Hint:** Think about what producers do, what consumers need, and the role of oxygen in respiration and decomposition.
