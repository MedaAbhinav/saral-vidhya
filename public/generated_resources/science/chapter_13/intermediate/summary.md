## Our Environment: Key Concepts for Class 10 CBSE

This chapter introduces the concept of **ecosystems**, which are formed by the interaction of all living organisms (biotic components: producers, consumers, decomposers) and their physical surroundings (abiotic components: temperature, rainfall, soil). Ecosystems can be natural (forests, ponds) or artificial (gardens, aquariums).

**Producers**, like green plants, create food through photosynthesis. **Consumers** obtain energy by eating other organisms, including herbivores (plant-eaters), carnivores (meat-eaters), and omnivores (eating both). **Decomposers**, such as bacteria and fungi, break down dead organic matter, recycling nutrients back into the environment.

A **food chain** illustrates the flow of energy as organisms feed on one another, with each step being a **trophic level**. Producers form the first trophic level. The interconnectedness of multiple food chains creates a **food web**. Understanding these interactions is crucial for maintaining the balance of nature.
