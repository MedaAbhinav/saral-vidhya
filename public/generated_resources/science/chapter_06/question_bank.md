### Section 2: Short Answer Questions (2-3 Marks)

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions concisely.

1.  **Define a reflex action. Give an example and explain why it is important.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A reflex action is a rapid, involuntary, and automatic response to a stimulus, occurring without conscious thought. Example: Pulling your hand away from a hot object. It's important for protecting the body from harm by responding quickly to dangerous situations.

2.  **Describe the structure of a neuron and its function in transmitting nerve impulses.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A neuron consists of a cell body, dendrites, and an axon. Dendrites receive signals, the cell body processes them, and the axon transmits the electrical impulse away from the cell body. This impulse is then converted to a chemical signal at the synapse to communicate with other cells.

3.  **What is a synapse? Explain its role in the transmission of nerve impulses between neurons.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A synapse is the junction between two neurons or between a neuron and an effector cell (like a muscle or gland). At the synapse, the electrical impulse in the presynaptic neuron triggers the release of neurotransmitters, which diffuse across the synaptic cleft and bind to receptors on the postsynaptic neuron, initiating a new electrical impulse.

4.  **Distinguish between voluntary and involuntary actions.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Voluntary actions are those that we consciously control, like walking or writing. Involuntary actions are those that occur automatically without our conscious control, such as breathing, heartbeats, and reflex actions.

5.  **What are the components of the nervous system involved in a reflex arc?**
    *   **Model Answer:** A reflex arc typically involves a receptor (detects stimulus), a sensory neuron (transmits impulse to CNS), an integration center (spinal cord or brainstem), a motor neuron (transmits impulse from CNS to effector), and an effector (muscle or gland that responds).

6.  **Explain the role of receptors in the nervous system.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Receptors are specialized cells or nerve endings that detect specific stimuli from the internal or external environment. Examples include photoreceptors in the eyes, phonoreceptors in the ears, olfactory receptors in the nose, gustatory receptors in the tongue, and thermoreceptors for heat. They convert these stimuli into electrical impulses.

7.  **What is the importance of the spinal cord in reflex actions?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The spinal cord acts as the primary center for many reflex arcs. It allows for rapid processing and response to stimuli without the direct involvement of the brain, thus enabling faster reactions to protect the body from potential harm.

8.  **How does the nervous system help in coordinating movements?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The nervous system coordinates movements by receiving sensory information from the body, processing it in the brain or spinal cord, and then sending signals through motor neurons to the muscles. This precise communication ensures that muscles contract and relax in the correct sequence and with the appropriate force for smooth and purposeful movement.

9.  **Briefly explain the function of dendrites and axons in a neuron.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Dendrites are branched extensions of a neuron that receive nerve impulses (signals) from other neurons or sensory receptors and transmit them towards the cell body. The axon is a long projection of a neuron that carries nerve impulses away from the cell body to other neurons, muscles, or glands.

10. **What is meant by "tropism" in plants? Give an example.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Tropism is the directional growth of a plant in response to an external stimulus. Example: Phototropism is the growth of a plant towards a light source, driven by the hormone auxin.

### Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

**Instructions:** Provide detailed answers with appropriate diagrams or outlines where necessary.

1.  **Describe the pathway of a nerve impulse from the point of detection of a stimulus to the response. Explain the concept of a reflex arc with a suitable diagram.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** Briefly explain the role of the nervous system in detecting stimuli and coordinating responses.
        *   **Detection of Stimulus:** Receptors in sense organs detect stimuli (e.g., heat, light).
        *   **Generation of Impulse:** Stimulus causes a chemical change, generating an electrical impulse in the sensory neuron's dendrite.
        *   **Transmission through Neuron:** Impulse travels along the axon to the axon terminal.
        *   **Synaptic Transmission:** At the synapse, chemical neurotransmitters are released, triggering an impulse in the next neuron.
        *   **Reflex Arc:**
            *   Definition and purpose of reflex arc.
            *   Pathway: Receptor -> Sensory Neuron -> Spinal Cord (Integration Center) -> Motor Neuron -> Effector (Muscle/Gland).
            *   **Diagram:** Labelled diagram of a reflex arc showing the components and the direction of impulse flow.
        *   **Response:** Effector carries out the action.
        *   **Conclusion:** Emphasize the speed and efficiency of reflex actions.

2.  **Explain the structure of a neuron and the process of transmission of nerve impulses, including the role of synapses.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Structure of a Neuron:**
            *   **Dendrites:** Branched extensions, receive signals.
            *   **Cell Body (Soma):** Contains nucleus, processes signals.
            *   **Axon:** Long projection, transmits signals away from cell body.
            *   **Axon Terminal:** End of axon, releases neurotransmitters.
        *   **Transmission of Nerve Impulses:**
            *   **Resting Potential:** Neuron's membrane is polarized.
            *   **Stimulation:** Stimulus causes depolarization, creating an action potential (electrical impulse).
            *   **Propagation:** Impulse travels along the axon.
            *   **Synapse:**
                *   **Description:** Junction between neurons.
                *   **Chemical Transmission:** Electrical impulse reaches axon terminal, releases neurotransmitters.
                *   **Neurotransmitters:** Cross synaptic cleft, bind to receptors on next neuron.
                *   **Postsynaptic Potential:** Causes excitation or inhibition in the next neuron.
        *   **Overall Flow:** Dendrite -> Cell Body -> Axon -> Axon Terminal -> Synapse -> Next Neuron.

3.  **What is the difference between the central nervous system and the peripheral nervous system? Describe the major components and functions of the central nervous system.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Definition and Distinction:**
            *   **Central Nervous System (CNS):** Brain and spinal cord. Central processing unit.
            *   **Peripheral Nervous System (PNS):** Nerves extending from CNS to all parts of the body. Relays information.
        *   **Central Nervous System (CNS):**
            *   **Brain:**
                *   **Forebrain (Cerebrum):** Thinking, memory, sensory perception, voluntary action.
                *   **Midbrain:** Relays sensory and motor information.
                *   **Hindbrain (Cerebellum, Pons, Medulla Oblongata):** Cerebellum - balance and coordination; Pons - relay; Medulla Oblongata - vital involuntary functions (breathing, heart rate).
            *   **Spinal Cord:**
                *   **Function:** Pathway for nerve impulses to and from the brain, center for reflex actions.
        *   **Functions of CNS:** Integration of information, coordination of activities, control of body functions, thought processes.

4.  **Discuss the role of hormones in plant growth and development, mentioning at least two types of plant hormones and their functions.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction to Hormones in Plants (Phytohormones):** Chemical substances produced in small amounts that regulate growth and development.
        *   **Auxins:**
            *   **Role:** Promote cell elongation, apical dominance, root formation, tropisms (e.g., phototropism).
            *   **Example:** Light stimulates auxin to move to the shaded side of a stem, causing cells there to elongate more, leading to bending towards light.
        *   **Gibberellins:**
            *   **Role:** Promote stem elongation, seed germination, flowering.
            *   **Example:** Breaking seed dormancy and initiating germination.
        *   *(Optional: Mention other hormones like Cytokinins, Abscisic Acid, Ethylene for a more comprehensive answer if time permits or if depth is required).*
        *   **Conclusion:** Hormones are crucial for coordinated growth and response to environmental cues in plants.

5.  **Explain how the human endocrine system controls and coordinates bodily functions. Give examples of two endocrine glands, their hormones, and their specific functions.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction to Endocrine System:** A system of glands that secrete hormones directly into the bloodstream to regulate various bodily functions.
        *   **Hormones:** Chemical messengers that travel through blood and act on target cells.
        *   **Control and Coordination:** Hormones provide slower but longer-lasting control compared to the nervous system.
        *   **Examples of Endocrine Glands and Hormones:**
            *   **Thyroid Gland:**
                *   **Hormone:** Thyroxine.
                *   **Function:** Regulates metabolism, growth, and development.
            *   **Adrenal Gland:**
                *   **Hormone:** Adrenaline (Epinephrine).
                *   **Function:** "Fight or flight" response - increases heart rate, breathing rate, blood pressure during stress or excitement.
            *   *(Optional: Mention Pituitary Gland - growth hormone, Islets of Langerhans - insulin/glucagon for diabetes control).*
        *   **Mechanism of Action:** Hormones bind to specific receptors on target cells, triggering specific responses.
        *   **Conclusion:** The endocrine system plays a vital role in maintaining homeostasis and coordinating long-term physiological processes.
