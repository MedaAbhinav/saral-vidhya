## Control and Coordination: A Summary

Living organisms need to **control and coordinate** their actions to respond effectively to environmental changes. This is achieved through two main systems: the **nervous system** and the **endocrine system**.

The **nervous system** uses specialized cells called **neurons** to transmit information as electrical impulses. These impulses travel from **receptors** (located in sense organs) to the **central nervous system** (brain and spinal cord) for processing, and then to **effectors** (muscles or glands) for a response. This rapid communication enables quick reactions like **reflex actions**, which are involuntary and protective movements.

The **endocrine system** uses **hormones**, chemical messengers produced by **glands**, which travel through the bloodstream to target cells. Hormones regulate slower, long-term processes like growth and metabolism. Together, these systems ensure that the body functions in a coordinated manner, allowing organisms to survive and thrive.
