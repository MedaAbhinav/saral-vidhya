## Control and Coordination: Easy Level Question Bank

This question bank focuses on basic recall and understanding of the chapter.

### 10 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) - 1 Mark Each

**Instructions:** Choose the best option for each question.

1.  Living organisms respond to changes in their environment. These changes are called:
    a) Growth
    b) Stimuli
    c) Movement
    d) Nutrition

2.  Which tissue is responsible for control and coordination in animals?
    a) Muscular tissue only
    b) Nervous tissue only
    c) Both nervous and muscular tissues
    d) Connective tissue

3.  Nerve cells are also called:
    a) Muscles
    b) Neurons
    c) Receptors
    d) Glands

4.  Where is the information acquired at the end of a nerve cell?
    a) Axon
    b) Cell body
    c) Dendrite
    d) Synapse

5.  An electrical impulse travels along which part of a neuron?
    a) Dendrite to axon
    b) Axon to dendrite
    c) Cell body to dendrite
    d) Synapse to cell body

6.  What is the gap between two neurons called?
    a) Synapse
    b) Axon terminal
    c) Dendritic tip
    d) Cell body

7.  A rapid, involuntary response to a stimulus is called a:
    a) Thinking process
    b) Reflex action
    c) Coordinated movement
    d) Growth

8.  Where are most reflex arcs formed in our body?
    a) Brain
    b) Nerves
    c) Spinal cord
    d) Sense organs

9.  Gustatory receptors are responsible for detecting:
    a) Smell
    b) Sight
    c) Taste
    d) Sound

10. Olfactory receptors are responsible for detecting:
    a) Smell
    b) Sight
    c) Taste
    d) Touch
