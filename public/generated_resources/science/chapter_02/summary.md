The chapter "Acids, Bases and Salts" defines acids as substances that are sour, corrosive, turn blue litmus red, and release H+ ions in aqueous solutions. Bases are bitter, soapy, turn red litmus blue, and release OH- ions. Indicators like litmus, phenolphthalein, and methyl orange show characteristic colour changes to identify them, while olfactory indicators change odour.

Exam-important reactions include:
*   Acids react with metals to produce salt and hydrogen gas.
*   Acids react with metal carbonates/bicarbonates to yield salt, carbon dioxide, and water.
*   Acids and bases react in a neutralization reaction to form salt and water.
*   Acids react with metallic oxides, and bases react with non-metallic oxides.

The pH scale measures the strength of acids and bases: pH < 7 is acidic, pH > 7 is basic, and pH = 7 is neutral. Understanding pH is vital for everyday applications. The chapter also covers various salts, their preparation, properties, and significant uses, including examples like common salt, baking soda, washing soda, bleaching powder, and Plaster of Paris.
