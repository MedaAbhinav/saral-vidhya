This chapter, "Light – Reflection and Refraction," focuses on the behavior of light, primarily treating it as a ray that travels in straight lines.  Key concepts include **reflection**, where light bounces off surfaces. The laws of reflection state that the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection, and all lie in the same plane. Plane mirrors form virtual, erect, same-sized, and laterally inverted images.

The chapter then introduces **spherical mirrors**, specifically **concave** (curved inwards) and **convex** (curved outwards). Understanding these mirrors and their properties is crucial for image formation.
