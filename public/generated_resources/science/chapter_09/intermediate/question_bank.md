### 2. Short Answer Questions (2–3 Marks)

1.  **Define the principal focus of a concave mirror. Where is the image formed when an object is placed at this point?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The principal focus (F) of a concave mirror is the point on the principal axis where rays of light, originally parallel to the principal axis, converge after reflection. When an object is placed at the principal focus of a concave mirror, the image is formed at infinity.

2.  **What is the difference between a concave mirror and a convex mirror? Give one use of each.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A concave mirror has a reflecting surface curved inwards, towards the center of the sphere it forms part of. A convex mirror has a reflecting surface curved outwards.
        *   **Use of Concave Mirror:** Used as shaving mirrors or dentists' mirrors to see larger images of the teeth.
        *   **Use of Convex Mirror:** Used as rear-view mirrors in vehicles because they give a wider field of view and form a virtual, erect, and diminished image.

3.  **State the laws of reflection. How are these laws applicable to spherical mirrors?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The laws of reflection are:
        (i) The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection ($\angle i = \angle r$).
        (ii) The incident ray, the reflected ray, and the normal to the surface at the point of incidence all lie in the same plane.
        These laws are universally applicable to all types of reflecting surfaces, including spherical mirrors, provided the "normal" is correctly identified at the point of incidence for the curved surface.

4.  **Derive the relationship between the focal length (f) and the radius of curvature (R) of a spherical mirror.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Consider a ray parallel to the principal axis of a concave mirror, incident at point A. After reflection, it passes through F. Draw the normal from C to A, which is along CA. Using geometry (parallel lines and transversal), it can be shown that $\angle CAP = \angle ACF$ (alternate interior angles and angle of incidence = angle of reflection). In $\triangle ACF$, since $\angle CAF = \angle ACF$, it is an isosceles triangle, so $AF = CF$. For paraxial rays, A is very close to P. Thus, $AF \approx PF$. Therefore, $PF = CF$. Since $PF = f$ and $CF = R - PF = R - f$, we get $f = R - f$, which simplifies to $2f = R$ or $f = R/2$.

5.  **What is meant by the term 'pole' and 'centre of curvature' of a spherical mirror?**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   **Pole (P):** The pole of a spherical mirror is the center of the reflecting surface. It lies on the surface of the mirror.
        *   **Centre of Curvature (C):** The center of curvature of a spherical mirror is the center of the sphere from which the reflecting surface is a part. It does not lie on the surface of the mirror itself.

6.  **Magnification produced by a plane mirror is +1. What does it signify?**
    *   **Model Answer:** A magnification of +1 signifies two things:
        (i) The magnitude of magnification is 1, which means the size of the image formed is exactly equal to the size of the object ($m = h_i/h_o = 1 \implies h_i = h_o$).
        (ii) The positive sign indicates that the image is erect and virtual, formed behind the mirror.

7.  **Distinguish between real and virtual images.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A real image is formed when rays of light actually converge at a point after reflection or refraction. Real images can be projected on a screen. A virtual image is formed when rays of light appear to diverge from a point after reflection or refraction. Virtual images cannot be projected on a screen; they can only be seen by looking into the mirror or lens.

8.  **A concave mirror is used as a shaving mirror. Why?**
    *   **Model Answer:** A concave mirror is used as a shaving mirror because when a person places their face within its focal length (between the pole and the principal focus), it forms a virtual, erect, and magnified image. This larger image allows the person to see finer details of their face clearly.

9.  **Explain why convex mirrors are preferred as rear-view mirrors in vehicles.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Convex mirrors are preferred as rear-view mirrors because they provide a much wider field of view compared to a plane mirror of the same size. They always form a virtual, erect, and diminished image, which allows the driver to see a larger area of the road behind the vehicle.

10. **If an object is placed at the center of curvature of a concave mirror, where is the image formed? Describe its characteristics.**
    *   **Model Answer:** If an object is placed at the center of curvature (C) of a concave mirror, the image is also formed at the center of curvature (C). The image formed is real, inverted, and of the same size as the object.

    *   **Outline Answer:**

            *   Position of Image: Between the pole (P) and the principal focus (F), behind the mirror.

            *   Size of Image: Diminished.

3.  **State the mirror formula and the magnification formula for spherical mirrors. Explain the sign convention used in these formulas.**

    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Mirror Formula:** $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$
        *   **Magnification Formula:** $m = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = -\frac{v}{u}$
        *   **Sign Convention (Cartesian Sign Convention):**
            *   All distances are measured from the pole (P) of the mirror.
            *   The principal axis is taken as the x-axis.
            *   Distances measured to the right of the origin (P) are positive.
            *   Distances measured to the left of the origin (P) are negative.
            *   Distances measured above the principal axis are positive.
            *   Distances measured below the principal axis are negative.
            *   Real objects are always placed to the left of the mirror (u is negative).
            *   Focal length (f) is positive for convex mirrors and negative for concave mirrors.
            *   Image distance (v) is positive for virtual images (behind the mirror) and negative for real images (in front of the mirror).
            *   Height of object ($h_o$) is usually taken as positive (erect).
            *   Height of image ($h_i$) is positive for erect images and negative for inverted images.

### 4. Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS) Questions

1.  A student is trying to obtain a sharp image of a distant tree on a screen using a spherical mirror. He places the screen at a distance of 40 cm from the mirror. He observes that the image is diminished and inverted.
    **(a) Identify the type of spherical mirror used.**
    **(b) Determine the focal length of the mirror.**
    **(c) What is the position of the object (the tree) relative to the mirror?**

    *   **Thinking Process:** The image is diminished and inverted, which is characteristic of the image formed by a convex mirror or a concave mirror when the object is placed beyond 2F. However, a convex mirror always forms a virtual, erect, and diminished image. Since the image is inverted, it must be a concave mirror. The screen is placed at 40 cm, so the image distance ($v$) is 40 cm. Since the image is real and inverted, $v$ is negative. The tree is distant, so the object distance ($u$) is very large (approaching infinity). If the object is very far, the image is formed close to the focus.
    *   **Solution Outline:**
        *   **(a)** The mirror is **concave**. (Reason: inverted and diminished image, suggesting real image; convex mirror forms virtual, erect, diminished image).
        *   **(b)** Since the object is a distant tree, we can assume it is at infinity. For an object at infinity, the image formed by a concave mirror is at its principal focus (F). Therefore, the image distance $v = -40$ cm (negative because it's a real image in front of the mirror). The focal length $f$ is equal to the image distance $v$ when $u = \infty$. So, the focal length $f = -40$ cm.
        *   **(c)** The position of the object (the tree) is at **infinity**.

2.  A ray of light strikes a spherical mirror such that it passes through the principal focus after reflection.
    **(a) What can you say about the incident ray's direction before reflection?**
    **(b) If the mirror is concave, and the image formed is virtual and erect, where is the object placed?**
    **(c) If the mirror is convex, and the object is placed at 20 cm, what is the focal length of the mirror, given that the image is formed at 15 cm behind the mirror?**

    *   **Thinking Process:** The statement "ray of light strikes a spherical mirror such that it passes through the principal focus after reflection" implies the incident ray was parallel to the principal axis. This is the definition of the principal focus for concave mirrors. For convex mirrors, reflected rays appear to come from the principal focus.
    *   **Solution Outline:**
        *   **(a)** The incident ray must have been **parallel to the principal axis** of the spherical mirror.
        *   **(b)** If the mirror is concave and the image formed is virtual and erect, the object must be placed **between the pole (P) and the principal focus (F)**.
        *   **(c)** For a convex mirror, object distance $u = -20$ cm (real object), image distance $v = +15$ cm (virtual image behind the mirror), focal length $f$ is what we need to find. Using the mirror formula: $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$.
            $\frac{1}{15} - \frac{1}{-20} = \frac{1}{f}$
            $\frac{1}{15} + \frac{1}{20} = \frac{1}{f}$
            $\frac{4+3}{60} = \frac{1}{f}$
            $\frac{7}{60} = \frac{1}{f}$
            $f = \frac{60}{7}$ cm $\approx 8.57$ cm.
            The focal length of the mirror is $\frac{60}{7}$ cm or approximately 8.57 cm.

3.  Two spherical mirrors, A and B, have the same focal length of 10 cm. Mirror A is a concave mirror, and Mirror B is a convex mirror.
    **(a) If an object is placed 30 cm in front of Mirror A, where is the image formed, and what is its nature?**
    **(b) If an object is placed 30 cm in front of Mirror B, where is the image formed, and what is its nature?**
    **(c) Compare the size of the image formed by Mirror A with the size of the object, and compare the size of the image formed by Mirror B with the size of the object.**

    *   **Thinking Process:** Apply the mirror formula and magnification formula using the correct sign conventions for concave and convex mirrors.
    *   **Solution Outline:**
        *   **(a) For Mirror A (Concave):** $f = -10$ cm, $u = -30$ cm.
            Mirror formula: $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$
            $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{-30} = \frac{1}{-10}$
            $\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{30} = -\frac{1}{10}$
            $\frac{1}{v} = -\frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{30} = \frac{-3-1}{30} = -\frac{4}{30} = -\frac{2}{15}$
            $v = -\frac{15}{2} = -7.5$ cm.
            Image is formed at **7.5 cm in front of the mirror**. Nature: **Real and inverted** (v is negative).
        *   **(b) For Mirror B (Convex):** $f = +10$ cm, $u = -30$ cm.
            Mirror formula: $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$
            $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{-30} = \frac{1}{10}$
            $\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{30} = \frac{1}{10}$
            $\frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{30} = \frac{3-1}{30} = \frac{2}{30} = \frac{1}{15}$
            $v = +15$ cm.
            Image is formed at **15 cm behind the mirror**. Nature: **Virtual and erect** (v is positive).
        *   **(c) Size Comparison:**
            **Mirror A (Concave):** Magnification $m = -\frac{v}{u} = -\frac{-7.5}{-30} = -\frac{7.5}{30} = -\frac{1}{4}$. The magnitude of magnification is $\frac{1}{4}$. This means the image is **diminished**, with its size being $\frac{1}{4}$ of the object's size.
            **Mirror B (Convex):** Magnification $m = -\frac{v}{u} = -\frac{+15}{-30} = -(-\frac{1}{2}) = +\frac{1}{2}$. The magnitude of magnification is $\frac{1}{2}$. This means the image is **diminished**, with its size being $\frac{1}{2}$ of the object's size.
            **Comparison:** The image formed by Mirror A is significantly more diminished (size is 1/4 of object) than the image formed by Mirror B (size is 1/2 of object).
