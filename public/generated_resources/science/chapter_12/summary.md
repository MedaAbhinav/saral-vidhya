This chapter explores the connection between electricity and magnetism. It highlights that an electric current flowing through a conductor creates a magnetic field around it, a discovery made by Hans Christian Oersted.

The concept of a **magnetic field** is introduced as the region around a magnet where its influence is felt. This field is visualized using **magnetic field lines**, which are imaginary lines that show the direction and strength of the magnetic field. These lines emerge from the north pole and enter the south pole of a magnet.

Key properties of magnetic field lines include:
*   They form closed loops.
*   They never intersect each other.
*   The density of field lines indicates the strength of the magnetic field.

The chapter also discusses the magnetic field produced by a current-carrying **straight conductor** and a **solenoid** (a coil of wire), and how these fields can be visualized.
