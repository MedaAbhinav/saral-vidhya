### 2. Short Answer Questions (2-3 Marks)

1.  **What is a magnetic field? How are magnetic field lines useful in representing it?**
    *   **Model Answer:** A magnetic field is the region around a magnet or a current-carrying conductor within which its magnetic effect can be experienced. Magnetic field lines are imaginary lines used to represent the magnetic field. They show the direction of the magnetic field and the relative strength of the field (where they are closer, the field is stronger).

2.  **State the properties of magnetic field lines.**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   Magnetic field lines emerge from the North pole and merge at the South pole of a magnet.
        *   Inside the magnet, field lines go from the South pole to the North pole, forming closed loops.
        *   The relative strength of the magnetic field is indicated by the degree of closeness of the field lines; they are crowded where the field is strong.
        *   No two magnetic field lines intersect each other.

3.  **Explain why a compass needle gets deflected when brought near a current-carrying conductor.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A current-carrying conductor produces a magnetic field around it. A compass needle is itself a small magnet. When placed near the magnetic field of the conductor, the compass needle experiences a force due to the interaction between its own magnetic field and the field produced by the current. This force causes the needle to align itself with the resultant magnetic field, leading to a deflection.

4.  **What is meant by the direction of the magnetic field? How is it conventionally defined?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The direction of the magnetic field at any point is the direction in which the North pole of a compass needle points when placed at that point. Conventionally, magnetic field lines are drawn to emerge from the North pole and enter the South pole of a magnet.

5.  **State the Right-Hand Rule (or Maxwell's Corkscrew Rule) and explain its utility.**
    *   **Model Answer:** The Right-Hand Rule states that if you grasp a current-carrying straight conductor with your right hand such that your thumb points in the direction of the electric current, then your fingers encircling the conductor will point in the direction of the magnetic field lines. It is useful for determining the direction of the magnetic field around a straight current-carrying conductor.

6.  **Why do magnetic field lines never intersect each other?**
    *   **Model Answer:** If two magnetic field lines were to intersect at a point, it would imply that the compass needle would point in two different directions at that same point. This is impossible because a compass needle can only point in one direction at any given location. Therefore, magnetic field lines never intersect.

7.  **Define magnetic field strength. What determines its relative strength?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Magnetic field strength is a vector quantity that has both magnitude and direction, representing the intensity of the magnetic field. The relative strength of the magnetic field is determined by the density or closeness of the magnetic field lines. Where field lines are crowded, the magnetic field is stronger, and where they are spread out, the field is weaker.

8.  **How does the direction of magnetic field change if the direction of current in a straight conductor is reversed?**
    *   **Model Answer:** If the direction of current in a straight conductor is reversed, the direction of the magnetic field produced around it also reverses. This can be observed through the deflection of a compass needle placed near the conductor; the needle will deflect in the opposite direction.

9.  **Briefly describe Hans Christian Oersted's discovery.**
    *   **Model Answer:** In 1820, Hans Christian Oersted accidentally discovered that a compass needle deflected when placed near an electric current-carrying wire. This observation demonstrated a direct relationship between electricity and magnetism, showing that an electric current produces a magnetic field.

10. **What is an electromagnet? How is it different from a permanent magnet?**
    *   **Model Answer:** An electromagnet is a type of magnet in which the magnetic field is produced by an electric current. Electromagnets are usually made by passing current through a coil of wire wrapped around a soft iron core. It differs from a permanent magnet in that its magnetism can be turned on and off by controlling the electric current, and its strength can be varied.

### 3. Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

1.  **Describe an activity to demonstrate that a magnetic field is produced around a current-carrying conductor. Explain the observations and draw a diagram.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Activity:** Set up an electric circuit with a straight thick copper wire, a battery, and a plug key. Place a compass near the wire.
        *   **Procedure:** Pass current through the wire and observe the compass needle's deflection. Reverse the direction of current and observe again.
        *   **Observations:** The compass needle deflects when current flows. The direction of deflection reverses when the direction of current is reversed.
        *   **Conclusion:** This shows that a current-carrying conductor produces a magnetic field around it, and the direction of this field depends on the direction of the current.
        *   **Diagram:** Include a labeled diagram of the circuit setup, showing the wire, compass, battery, and key, along with the direction of current and deflected needle.

2.  **Explain the concept of magnetic field lines. Describe how to draw magnetic field lines around a bar magnet and state their important properties.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Definition:** Magnetic field lines are imaginary lines representing the direction and strength of a magnetic field.
        *   **Drawing:** Describe the activity using a bar magnet, paper, iron filings, and a compass to plot the field lines. Mention marking points with a compass and joining them.
        *   **Properties:** List and explain the key properties:
            *   Emergence from North, merging at South.
            *   Closed curves (including inside the magnet).
            *   Direction inside the magnet (S to N).
            *   Relative strength indicated by closeness.
            *   Non-intersecting nature.
        *   **Diagram:** Include a diagram showing magnetic field lines around a bar magnet with arrows indicating direction.

3.  **Describe the magnetic field produced by a straight current-carrying conductor. Explain how its direction is determined using the Right-Hand Rule.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Nature of Field:** A straight current-carrying conductor produces a magnetic field in the form of concentric circles around the conductor.
        *   **Factors affecting strength:** Mention that the field strength depends on the current's magnitude and the distance from the conductor.
        *   **Direction determination:**
            *   State the Right-Hand Rule clearly.
            *   Explain how to apply it: thumb points in the direction of current, fingers curl in the direction of magnetic field lines.
        *   **Diagram:** Draw a straight conductor with current shown and concentric circles representing magnetic field lines, indicating the direction with arrows.

4.  **What is an electromagnet? Explain the factors on which the strength of an electromagnet depends and how it can be increased.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Definition:** Define electromagnet as a magnet created by an electric current flowing through a coil.
        *   **Construction:** Briefly mention the core (soft iron) and the coil.
        *   **Factors affecting strength:**
            *   Number of turns in the coil.
            *   Magnitude of current flowing through the coil.
            *   Nature of the core material.
        *   **Increasing Strength:** Explain how increasing the number of turns or increasing the current increases the strength.
        *   **Applications (briefly):** Mention a common application like electromagnets in cranes.

5.  **Discuss the magnetic effects of electric current. How can the direction of the magnetic field around a current-carrying wire be visualized and determined?**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Introduction:** State that electric current has magnetic effects, as discovered by Oersted.
        *   **Visualization/Determination of Field:**
            *   **Activity 12.1:** Briefly mention the experiment with a compass and a straight wire to show the effect.
            *   **Magnetic Field Lines:** Explain that the field forms concentric circles.
            *   **Right-Hand Rule:** Detail the application of the Right-Hand Rule to find the direction of the magnetic field.
            *   **Direction reversal:** Explain how reversing current reverses the field direction.
        *   **Diagram:** A simple diagram showing a current-carrying wire and concentric field lines with directional arrows, illustrating the Right-Hand Rule.
