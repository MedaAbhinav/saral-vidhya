```mermaid
mindmap
  root(Human Eye and Colourful World)
    (The Human Eye)
      (Structure)
        (Cornea)
          Transparent bulge on front surface
          Most refraction occurs here
        (Lens System)
          Crystalline lens
          Finer adjustment of focal length
          Forms inverted real image on retina
        (Retina)
          Light-sensitive screen
          Has light-sensitive cells
          Generates electrical signals
        (Iris)
          Dark muscular diaphragm
          Controls pupil size
        (Pupil)
          Regulates amount of light entering
        (Ciliary Muscles)
          Modify lens curvature
          Enable accommodation
        (Optic Nerves)
          Send electrical signals to brain
      (Function)
        Enables vision and perception of colours
        Like a camera
      (Power of Accommodation)
        Ability to adjust focal length
        For distant objects: Ciliary muscles relaxed, lens thin, focal length increases
        For nearby objects: Ciliary muscles contract, lens thicker, focal length decreases
      (Least Distance of Distinct Vision)
        Minimum distance for clear vision without strain
        Approx. 25 cm for young adult
      (Far Point)
        Farthest point for clear vision
        Infinity for a normal eye
      (Normal Vision Range)
        Objects between 25 cm and infinity can be seen clearly
      (Cataract)
        Crystalline lens becomes milky/cloudy (old age)
        Causes partial/complete loss of vision
        Corrected by surgery
    (Defects of Vision and Their Correction)
      (Refractive Defects)
        Caused by gradual loss of accommodation power
        Vision becomes blurred
      (Myopia (Near-sightedness))
        (Defect)
          Can see nearby objects clearly
          Cannot see distant objects clearly
          Image formed in front of retina
        (Cause)
          Excessive curvature of eye lens OR
          Elongation of eyeball
        (Correction)
          Concave lens
          Diverges light rays before entering eye
      (Hypermetropia (Far-sightedness))
        (Defect)
          Can see distant objects clearly
          Cannot see nearby objects clearly
          Image formed behind retina
        (Cause)
          Insufficient curvature of eye lens OR
          Shortening of eyeball
        (Correction)
          Convex lens
          Converges light rays before entering eye
      (Presbyopia)
        (Defect)
          Diminished power of accommodation with age
          Difficulty in seeing nearby objects
        (Cause)
          Ciliary muscles weaken, eye lens becomes less flexible
        (Correction)
          Bifocal lenses (concave for myopia, convex for hypermetropia)
    (Phenomena in Nature)
      (Dispersion of White Light)
        Splitting of white light into its constituent colours (VIBGYOR)
        By a prism
        Wavelength dependency of refraction
      (Rainbow)
        (Formation)
          Internal reflection and dispersion of sunlight by water droplets
        (Conditions)
          Sun behind the observer, rain in front
        (Colours)
          Arranged in order of wavelength (VIBGYOR)
      (Scattering of Light)
        (Rayleigh Scattering)
          Scattering of light by small particles (like air molecules)
          Intensity of scattered light is inversely proportional to the fourth power of wavelength (I ∝ 1/λ⁴)
        (Blue Colour of the Sky)
          Atmosphere scatters blue light (shorter wavelength) more than red light (longer wavelength)
        (Redness of the Sun at Sunrise/Sunset)
          Light travels through a longer path in atmosphere
          Blue light is scattered away, leaving red light to reach our eyes
      (Tyndall Effect)
        Scattering of light by larger particles (like dust, smoke, milk)
        Makes the path of light visible
        Observed in colloidal solutions
```
