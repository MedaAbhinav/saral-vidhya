### 2. Short Answer Questions (2-3 Marks)

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions concisely.

1.  **Why is diffusion insufficient to meet the oxygen requirements of multicellular organisms like humans?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Diffusion is a slow process that relies on the concentration gradient. In multicellular organisms, the body size is large, and cells are located deep within the tissues, far from the external environment. The surface area to volume ratio is also small. Therefore, diffusion alone cannot supply enough oxygen to all the cells or remove waste products efficiently within the required time. Specialized transport systems are needed.

2.  **What criteria do we use to decide whether something is alive?**
    *   **Model Answer:** We use several criteria, including movement (though not always visible externally), growth, respiration (molecular movement for energy), response to stimuli, reproduction, and metabolism (maintenance of organized structures). While visible movement is a common indicator, invisible molecular movement is also crucial for life.

3.  **What are outside raw materials used for by an organism?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Outside raw materials (like food and oxygen) are used by organisms for two main purposes:
        *   **Energy:** To carry out life processes and maintain organized structures.
        *   **Growth and Repair:** To build new molecules, grow, develop, and repair damaged tissues.

4.  **What processes would you consider essential for maintaining life?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Essential life processes for maintaining life include:
        *   **Nutrition:** Obtaining and utilizing food for energy and raw materials.
        *   **Respiration:** Releasing energy from food.
        *   **Transport:** Moving nutrients, oxygen, and waste products throughout the body.
        *   **Excretion:** Removing metabolic waste products.

5.  **Differentiate between autotrophic and heterotrophic modes of nutrition.**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   **Autotrophic nutrition:** Organisms (like green plants) prepare their own food using inorganic substances (CO2 and water) in the presence of sunlight and chlorophyll. They are self-feeders.
        *   **Heterotrophic nutrition:** Organisms (like animals and fungi) cannot prepare their own food and depend on autotrophs directly or indirectly for their food. They consume complex organic substances.

6.  **Explain the role of chlorophyll in photosynthesis.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Chlorophyll is a green pigment found in chloroplasts. Its primary role in photosynthesis is to absorb light energy from the sun. This absorbed light energy is then used to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose (food) and oxygen.

7.  **What is the function of villi in the small intestine?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Villi are finger-like projections lining the inner wall of the small intestine. Their function is to significantly increase the surface area available for the absorption of digested food molecules (like glucose, amino acids, fatty acids) into the bloodstream.

8.  **Describe the process of respiration in simple terms.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Respiration is a life process where organisms break down food molecules (like glucose) to release energy. This energy is then used to carry out various life activities. In aerobic respiration, oxygen is used, and carbon dioxide and water are produced as by-products.

9.  **What is the importance of enzymes in digestion?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up the rate of chemical reactions. In digestion, enzymes break down large, complex food molecules into smaller, simpler molecules that can be absorbed and utilized by the body. Without enzymes, digestion would be too slow to sustain life.

10. **Name the two main transport tissues in plants and state their functions.**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   **Xylem:** Transports water and dissolved minerals from the roots to the rest of the plant.
        *   **Phloem:** Transports the prepared food (sugars) from the leaves to other parts of the plant where it is needed for storage or growth.

### 3. Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

**Instructions:** Provide detailed answers to the following questions.

1.  **Describe the process of photosynthesis in detail. Include the raw materials, conditions, and the overall chemical equation.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Definition:** Process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize foods with the help of chlorophyll pigment.
        *   **Raw Materials:** Carbon dioxide (from atmosphere) and Water (absorbed from soil).
        *   **Conditions:** Sunlight (energy source) and Chlorophyll (pigment to absorb light).
        *   **Site:** Chloroplasts in plant cells, particularly in leaves.
        *   **Steps (briefly):** Light-dependent reactions (water splitting, oxygen release, ATP & NADPH formation) and Light-independent reactions (carbon fixation, glucose synthesis).
        *   **Products:** Glucose (food) and Oxygen (released).
        *   **Equation:** $6\text{CO}_2 + 6\text{H}_2\text{O} \xrightarrow{\text{Sunlight, Chlorophyll}} \text{C}_6\text{H}_{12}\text{O}_6 + 6\text{O}_2$.
        *   **Significance:** Basis of most food chains, provides oxygen for respiration.

2.  **Explain the process of digestion in humans, from the mouth to the small intestine. Mention the role of different organs and secretions.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Mouth:** Ingestion, mechanical digestion (chewing), chemical digestion (salivary amylase breaks starch into maltose).
        *   **Oesophagus:** Peristalsis moves food to the stomach.
        *   **Stomach:** Mechanical churning, acidic environment (HCl denatures proteins, kills bacteria), Pepsin (enzyme) breaks down proteins into smaller peptides.
        *   **Small Intestine (Duodenum):** Receives secretions from liver (bile) and pancreas (pancreatic juice).
            *   **Bile:** Emulsifies fats, making them easier to digest.
            *   **Pancreatic Juice:** Contains amylase (starch), trypsin (proteins), and lipase (fats).
        *   **Small Intestine (Jejunum & Ileum):** Final digestion and absorption of digested nutrients. Intestinal enzymes complete the breakdown of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats. Villi increase surface area for absorption.

3.  **Describe the double circulation of blood in humans. Why is it called double circulation, and what are its advantages?**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Definition:** Blood passes through the heart twice for one complete cycle.
        *   **Two Circuits:**
            *   **Pulmonary Circulation:** Deoxygenated blood from body $\to$ Right Atrium $\to$ Right Ventricle $\to$ Lungs (gets oxygenated) $\to$ Left Atrium.
            *   **Systemic Circulation:** Oxygenated blood from Lungs $\to$ Left Atrium $\to$ Left Ventricle $\to$ Body (delivers oxygen) $\to$ Right Atrium.
        *   **Why "Double":** Blood goes through the heart twice in a complete circuit.
        *   **Advantages:**
            *   **Efficiency:** Separates oxygenated and deoxygenated blood, allowing for efficient delivery of oxygen to tissues.
            *   **High Metabolic Rate:** Supports the high energy demands of warm-blooded animals like humans.
            *   **Precise Regulation:** Allows for better regulation of body temperature.

4.  **Explain the process of excretion in humans. Mention the main organs involved and the role of the kidneys.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Definition:** Process of removing metabolic waste products from the body.
        *   **Main Organs:** Kidneys, Ureters, Urinary Bladder, Urethra.
        *   **Role of Kidneys:**
            *   Filter blood to remove waste products like urea, excess salts, and water.
            *   Form urine.
            *   Maintain water-salt balance (osmoregulation).
            *   Maintain pH balance.
        *   **Process:** Blood enters the kidneys, undergoes filtration (glomerulus), reabsorption of useful substances (tubules), and secretion of waste products to form urine.
        *   **Ureters:** Carry urine from kidneys to the urinary bladder.
        *   **Urinary Bladder:** Stores urine.
        *   **Urethra:** Expels urine from the body.

5.  **Discuss the importance of transportation of materials in plants. Explain the roles of xylem and phloem in this process.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **Importance:** Plants need to transport water, minerals, and food to all their cells for survival, growth, and metabolic activities.
        *   **Xylem:**
            *   **Function:** Transports water and dissolved minerals from roots to leaves and other aerial parts.
            *   **Mechanism:** Transpiration pull (evaporation of water from leaves creates a negative pressure that pulls water up), adhesion and cohesion of water molecules.
            *   **Nature:** Generally dead tissue, forms continuous channels.
        *   **Phloem:**
            *   **Function:** Transports food (sugars produced during photosynthesis) from leaves to all other parts of the plant (storage organs, growing regions).
            *   **Mechanism:** Translocation, driven by a pressure gradient from source (leaves) to sink (other parts).
            *   **Nature:** Living tissue, consists of sieve tubes, companion cells.

### 4. Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS) Questions

1.  **If a person has damaged kidney function, how would this affect the overall functioning of their body, considering other life processes like respiration and nutrition?**
    *   **Thinking Prompt:** Think about what the kidneys remove and what happens if those substances build up. How does that waste accumulation impact other bodily functions that rely on a stable internal environment?

2.  **Some plants release oxygen during the day but absorb oxygen and release carbon dioxide during the night. Explain the two different processes that are occurring.**
    *   **Thinking Prompt:** Consider the primary process that happens during the day that requires sunlight and the process that happens constantly in living organisms.

3.  **Why is it important for the heart to be a muscular organ that pumps blood continuously? What would happen if the heart stopped pumping for even a few minutes?**
    *   **Thinking Prompt:** Consider what blood carries. What would be the immediate consequence for the cells if they stopped receiving these essential components and if waste products were not removed?
