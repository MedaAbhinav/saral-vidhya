## NCERT Class 10 Science: Electricity

---

### Course Description

This comprehensive course delves into the fundamental principles of electricity, as prescribed by the NCERT Class 10 Science syllabus. Students will explore the concepts of electric current, potential difference, resistance, and their interrelationships. The course will further investigate the practical applications of these principles, including electric power, heating effects, and the functioning of common electrical devices. Through engaging explanations, illustrative examples, and problem-solving exercises, students will build a strong conceptual understanding of electricity, essential for their academic success and future scientific endeavors.

---

### Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1.  **Define and explain** key concepts such as electric charge, electric current, electric potential, and potential difference.
2.  **Understand and apply** Ohm's Law to solve problems involving voltage, current, and resistance in simple circuits.
3.  **Explain and differentiate** between conductors and insulators, and understand the factors affecting resistance.
4.  **Analyze and calculate** the power consumed by electrical appliances and the energy dissipated due to heating effects.
5.  **Describe** the functioning of common electrical devices like electric heaters, electric irons, and electric motors.
6.  **Illustrate and interpret** circuit diagrams using standard symbols and analyze simple series and parallel circuits.

---

### Target Audience

This course is specifically designed for students of **NCERT Class 10** who are studying Science. It is also beneficial for any student seeking to reinforce their understanding of basic electrical concepts.

---

### Prerequisites

*   Basic understanding of fundamental physics concepts from previous classes.
*   Familiarity with simple mathematical operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division).

---

### Module Breakdown

**Module 1: Electric Current and Charge**

*   Electric Charge and its properties
*   Electric Field and Potential (Introduction)
*   Electric Current: Definition and Unit (Ampere)
*   Direction of Electric Current
*   Electric Circuit and Symbols

**Module 2: Electric Potential Difference and Ohm's Law**

*   Electric Potential and Potential Difference
*   Ohm's Law: Statement and Explanation
*   Factors Affecting Resistance (Resistivity)
*   Series Combination of Resistors
*   Parallel Combination of Resistors

**Module 3: Heating Effect of Electric Current**

*   Joule's Law of Heating
*   Electric Power: Definition and Unit (Watt)
*   Relationship between Power, Current, Voltage, and Resistance
*   Commercial Units of Energy (Kilowatt-hour)

**Module 4: Applications of Electricity**

*   Electric Heating Devices (e.g., Electric Heater, Electric Iron)
*   Electric Motor: Principle and Function (Basic Introduction)
*   Electric Generator: Principle and Function (Basic Introduction)
*   Household Electric Circuits (Brief Overview)

---

### Assessment Plan

The assessment will be a combination of the following:

*   **Module Quizzes (40%):** Short quizzes at the end of each module to assess comprehension of key concepts and definitions.
*   **Problem-Solving Assignments (30%):** Regular assignments involving numerical problems and conceptual questions to test application of laws and principles.
*   **Final Assessment (30%):** A comprehensive assessment covering all modules, including multiple-choice questions, short answer questions, and numerical problems.

---

### Duration

**Total Duration:** 4 Weeks (approx. 16-20 hours of learning, including lectures, practice, and assessments)

---

### Difficulty Level

**Beginner to Intermediate**

This course is designed to build foundational knowledge. While it covers core scientific principles, the depth of exploration is tailored for a Class 10 level, making it accessible for students with no prior specialized knowledge in electricity.
