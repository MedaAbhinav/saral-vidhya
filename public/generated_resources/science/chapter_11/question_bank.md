### 2. Short Answer Questions (2-3 Marks)

1.  **Define electric current. How is it measured?**
    **Model Answer:** Electric current is defined as the rate of flow of electric charges through a conductor. It is expressed by the amount of charge flowing through a particular area in unit time. Mathematically, $I = Q/t$. It is measured by an instrument called an ammeter, which is always connected in series in the circuit.

2.  **What is an electric circuit? What happens if the circuit is broken?**
    **Model Answer:** An electric circuit is a continuous and closed path of an electric current. If the circuit is broken anywhere, the path becomes discontinuous, and the current stops flowing. Consequently, electrical devices connected in the circuit (like a bulb) will not function.

3.  **Define potential difference. What is its SI unit?**
    **Model Answer:** Potential difference between two points in an electric circuit is defined as the work done to move a unit charge from one point to the other. Its SI unit is the Volt (V). 1 Volt is the potential difference when 1 Joule of work is done to move 1 Coulomb of charge.

4.  **State the convention for the direction of electric current.**
    **Model Answer:** Conventionally, the direction of electric current is taken as the direction of flow of positive charges. In metallic conductors, where electrons (negative charges) flow, the direction of electric current is taken as opposite to the direction of flow of electrons.

5.  **Calculate the number of electrons constituting one coulomb of charge. (Given the charge of an electron = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C).**
    **Model Answer:**
    Charge of an electron ($e$) = $-1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C.
    Let $n$ be the number of electrons constituting a charge of 1 C.
    Total charge $Q = n \times e$
    $1 \, \text{C} = n \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C})$
    $n = \frac{1 \, \text{C}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}} = \frac{1}{1.6} \times 10^{19} = 0.625 \times 10^{19} = 6.25 \times 10^{18}$ electrons.
    Therefore, approximately $6.25 \times 10^{18}$ electrons constitute one coulomb of charge.

6.  **How much energy is given to each coulomb of charge passing through a 6 V battery?**
    **Model Answer:**
    Potential difference (V) = 6 V
    Charge (Q) = 1 C
    Work done (W) = V × Q
    W = 6 V × 1 C = 6 J.
    The energy given to each coulomb of charge passing through a 6 V battery is 6 Joules.

7.  **What is the function of a cell in an electric circuit?**
    **Model Answer:** A cell (or battery) is a device that maintains a potential difference across the terminals of a conductor. This potential difference sets the charges in motion, producing an electric current in the circuit, and thus sustaining the current. It converts stored chemical energy into electrical energy.

8.  **What is the difference between a voltmeter and an ammeter in terms of their connection in a circuit?**
    **Model Answer:** An ammeter is always connected in series in the circuit to measure the current flowing through that part of the circuit. A voltmeter is always connected in parallel across the two points between which the potential difference is to be measured.

9.  **If a charge of 500 C passes through a conductor in 10 seconds, what is the electric current?**
    **Model Answer:**
    Charge (Q) = 500 C
    Time (t) = 10 s
    Current (I) = Q / t
    I = 500 C / 10 s = 50 A.
    The electric current is 50 Amperes.

10. **Draw the symbol for an open plug key and a wire joint.**
    **Model Answer:**
    *   **Open Plug Key:** $\text{-----}\quad\text{-----}$ (A gap between two points)
    *   **Wire Joint:** $\bullet$ (A dot where wires meet)

### 3. Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

1.  **Explain the concept of electric current. Define its SI unit and state its relationship with charge and time. Also, mention the conventional direction of current.**
    **Outline Answer:**
    *   Define electric current as the rate of flow of charge.
    *   Provide the formula $I = Q/t$.
    *   Define the SI unit of current as Ampere (A) and state that 1 A = 1 C/s.
    *   Explain that small currents are measured in mA and µA.
    *   Explain the conventional direction of current (flow of positive charge) and its relation to electron flow (negative charge).
    *   Mention the instrument used to measure current (ammeter) and its connection (series).

2.  **Define electric potential difference. Explain its role in maintaining current in a conductor. Define its SI unit and provide the formula relating potential difference, work done, and charge.**
    **Outline Answer:**
    *   Define potential difference as work done per unit charge to move a charge between two points.
    *   Provide the formula $V = W/Q$.
    *   Explain that potential difference creates an "electric pressure" or "electric pressure difference" that causes charges to flow, similar to water flow in a tube.
    *   Define the SI unit of potential difference as Volt (V) and state that 1 V = 1 J/C.
    *   Mention that a cell or battery is a source of potential difference and expends energy to maintain it.
    *   Mention the instrument used to measure potential difference (voltmeter) and its connection (parallel).

3.  **What do you understand by the term 'electric circuit'? Draw a schematic diagram of a simple electric circuit including a cell, a bulb, an ammeter, and a closed plug key. Explain the function of each component in the diagram.**
    **Outline Answer:**
    *   Define electric circuit as a continuous and closed path for current.
    *   Draw the schematic diagram using standard symbols:
        *   Cell (Battery)
        *   Bulb
        *   Ammeter
        *   Closed Plug Key (Switch)
        *   Connecting Wires
    *   Explain the function of each component:
        *   Cell: Provides potential difference to drive current.
        *   Bulb: An electrical device that glows when current passes through it.
        *   Ammeter: Measures the electric current.
        *   Closed Plug Key: Completes the circuit, allowing current to flow.

4.  **Explain the different symbols used in circuit diagrams. Draw the symbols for the following components: a battery, a resistor, and an open switch.**
    **Outline Answer:**
    *   Explain the need for schematic diagrams and conventional symbols for easy representation of circuits.
    *   List and draw the symbols for common components (e.g., cell, battery, plug key (open/closed), bulb, resistor, rheostat, ammeter, voltmeter).
    *   Specifically draw and label the symbols for:
        *   Battery (combination of cells)
        *   Resistor (fixed)
        *   Open Switch (Plug key)

5.  **A current of 0.5 A is drawn by a filament of an electric bulb for 10 minutes. Calculate the amount of electric charge that flows through the circuit and the number of electrons constituting this charge. (Charge of an electron = $1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C).**
    **Outline Answer:**
    *   State the given values: Current (I) = 0.5 A, Time (t) = 10 minutes.
    *   Convert time to seconds: t = 10 min * 60 s/min = 600 s.
    *   Calculate the charge (Q) using $Q = I \times t$. Show the calculation.
    *   State the charge of an electron ($e$).
    *   Calculate the number of electrons (n) using $n = Q/e$. Show the calculation.
    *   State the final answers for both charge and number of electrons.
