Here is an easy-level question bank for NCERT Class 10 Science – "Metals and Non-metals", designed for beginners.

## Metals and Non-metals: Easy Question Bank

### 1. Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) - 1 Mark Each

Choose the correct option for each question.

1.  Which of the following is a characteristic property of metals?
    (a) Dull surface
    (b) Poor conductors of heat
    (c) Lustrous surface (shining)
    (d) Break easily into pieces

2.  The property of metals that allows them to be beaten into thin sheets is called:
    (a) Ductility
    (b) Malleability
    (c) Sonority
    (d) Conductivity

3.  Metals generally produce which type of oxides when dissolved in water?
    (a) Acidic
    (b) Basic
    (c) Neutral
    (d) None of these

4.  Which of the following is an example of a non-metal?
    (a) Iron
    (b) Copper
    (c) Sulphur
    (d) Aluminium

5.  Mercury is a unique metal because it is a ______ at room temperature.
    (a) Solid
    (b) Gas
    (c) Liquid
    (d) Powder

6.  Which property allows metals to be drawn into thin wires?
    (a) Malleability
    (b) Brittleness
    (c) Ductility
    (d) Hardness

7.  School bells are made of metals because metals are:
    (a) Non-sonorous
    (b) Sonorous
    (c) Brittle
    (d) Poor conductors

8.  Which of these non-metals is lustrous (shining)?
    (a) Coal
    (b) Oxygen
    (c) Iodine
    (d) Hydrogen

9.  Metals are generally good conductors of:
    (a) Sound only
    (b) Heat and electricity
    (c) Light only
    (d) None of these

10. Which of the following metals can be easily cut with a knife?
    (a) Iron
    (b) Copper
    (c) Sodium
    (d) Aluminium

### 2. Very Short Answer Questions - 1 Mark Each

Answer each question in one line.

1.  What is the shiny appearance of metals called?
2.  Name one metal that is liquid at room temperature.
3.  What is the property of metals to be drawn into thin wires called?
4.  Give one example of a non-metal.
5.  What kind of sound do metals produce when struck?

### 3. Fill-in-the-Blanks - 1 Mark Each

Fill in the blanks with the appropriate word.

1.  Metals are generally ________ conductors of heat and electricity.
2.  The property of being hammered into thin sheets is called ________.
3.  Bromine is a non-metal that is a ________ at room temperature.
4.  Metals have ________ melting and boiling points.
5.  Diamond, an allotrope of carbon, is very ________.

## Answer Key

### 1. MCQs

1.  (c) Lustrous surface (shining)
2.  (b) Malleability
3.  (b) Basic
4.  (c) Sulphur
5.  (c) Liquid
6.  (c) Ductility
7.  (b) Sonorous
8.  (c) Iodine
9.  (b) Heat and electricity
10. (c) Sodium

### 2. Very Short Answer Questions

1.  Metallic lustre
2.  Mercury
3.  Ductility
4.  Carbon (or Sulphur, Iodine, Oxygen, Hydrogen)
5.  A ringing sound (or Sonorous sound)
