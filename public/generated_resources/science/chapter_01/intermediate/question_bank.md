### Section 2: Short Answer Questions (2–3 Marks Each)

1.  **What is meant by a chemical reaction? State any three observations that help us to determine if a chemical reaction has taken place.**
    *   **Model Answer:** A chemical reaction is a process in which one or more substances change to form new substances with different properties. The observations that indicate a chemical reaction has taken place are:
        *   Change in state (e.g., solid to gas)
        *   Change in colour
        *   Evolution of a gas
        *   Change in temperature (either heating or cooling)

2.  **State the Law of Conservation of Mass. Why is it important to balance a chemical equation?**
    *   **Model Answer:** The Law of Conservation of Mass states that mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. It means that the total mass of the elements present in the reactants must be equal to the total mass of the elements present in the products. Balancing a chemical equation is important to ensure that the number of atoms of each element remains the same on both sides of the equation, thereby adhering to the Law of Conservation of Mass.

3.  **Differentiate between a physical change and a chemical change with one example of each.**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   **Physical Change:** In a physical change, the substance changes its form or appearance, but not its chemical identity. No new substance is formed. These changes are usually temporary.
            *   *Example:* Melting of ice into water. Water molecules remain $H_2O$ in both solid and liquid states.
        *   **Chemical Change:** In a chemical change, a new substance with entirely new properties is formed. These changes are usually permanent.
            *   *Example:* Burning of wood. Wood turns into ash, carbon dioxide, and water vapor, which are new substances.

4.  **Write a balanced chemical equation for the following reaction: Zinc metal reacts with dilute sulphuric acid to form zinc sulphate and hydrogen gas.**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   Unbalanced equation: $Zn + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow ZnSO_4 + H_2$
        *   Balancing:
            *   Zn: 1 on LHS, 1 on RHS. Balanced.
            *   H: 2 on LHS, 2 on RHS. Balanced.
            *   S: 1 on LHS, 1 on RHS. Balanced.
            *   O: 4 on LHS, 4 on RHS. Balanced.
        *   **Balanced equation:** $Zn(s) + H_2SO_4(aq) \rightarrow ZnSO_4(aq) + H_2(g)$
        *(Note: State symbols are optional for 2-mark, but good practice for higher marks or understanding)*

5.  **What happens when carbon dioxide gas is passed through lime water? Write the balanced chemical equation for the reaction.**
    *   **Model Answer:** When carbon dioxide gas is passed through lime water (calcium hydroxide solution), it turns milky due to the formation of a white precipitate of calcium carbonate.
        *   **Balanced equation:** $Ca(OH)_2(aq) + CO_2(g) \rightarrow CaCO_3(s) + H_2O(l)$

6.  **Identify the types of reactions in the following examples:**
    (a) $2Fe + Cl_2 \rightarrow 2FeCl_3$
    (b) $Pb(NO_3)_2(aq) + 2KI(aq) \rightarrow PbI_2(s) + 2KNO_3(aq)$
    *   **Model Answer:**
        (a) This is a **combination reaction** (or synthesis reaction) where two elements combine to form a compound. It can also be considered a **redox reaction**.
        (b) This is a **double displacement reaction** (specifically, a precipitation reaction because a precipitate is formed).

7.  **Write word equations and then balanced chemical equations for the following reactions:**
    (a) Magnesium reacts with hydrochloric acid to produce magnesium chloride and hydrogen gas.
    (b) Hydrogen gas combines with nitrogen gas to form ammonia gas.
    *   **Model Answer:**
        (a) **Word equation:** Magnesium + Hydrochloric acid → Magnesium chloride + Hydrogen gas
            **Balanced chemical equation:** $Mg(s) + 2HCl(aq) \rightarrow MgCl_2(aq) + H_2(g)$
        (b) **Word equation:** Hydrogen gas + Nitrogen gas → Ammonia gas
            **Balanced chemical equation:** $3H_2(g) + N_2(g) \rightarrow 2NH_3(g)$

8.  **Give two examples of decomposition reactions.**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        (a) Decomposition of calcium carbonate by heating: $CaCO_3(s) \xrightarrow{heat} CaO(s) + CO_2(g)$
        (b) Decomposition of water by electrolysis: $2H_2O(l) \xrightarrow{electrolysis} 2H_2(g) + O_2(g)$

9.  **What is meant by 'oxidised' and 'reduced' in terms of electron transfer?**
    *   **Model Answer:**
        *   **Oxidised:** A substance is oxidised when it loses electrons.
        *   **Reduced:** A substance is reduced when it gains electrons.
        *(This relates to the broader definition of redox reactions beyond oxygen/hydrogen gain/loss).*

10. **Explain why respiration is considered an exothermic reaction.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Respiration is the process where glucose is broken down in the body in the presence of oxygen to release energy. This energy is vital for living organisms. Since energy is released during this process, respiration is classified as an exothermic reaction. The overall reaction can be simplified as: $C_6H_{12}O_6(aq) + 6O_2(g) \rightarrow 6CO_2(g) + 6H_2O(l) + Energy$.

### Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Marks Each)

1.  **(a) What is a balanced chemical equation? Explain with the help of an example, the steps involved in balancing a chemical equation.**
    **(b) Why is it necessary to balance chemical equations?**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **(a)** Define balanced chemical equation (number of atoms of each element same on both sides).
        *   Explain the need for balancing (Law of Conservation of Mass).
        *   Use an example like $Fe + H_2O \rightarrow Fe_3O_4 + H_2$.
        *   Detail the step-by-step balancing process:
            *   Draw boxes around formulas.
            *   List atoms of each element on LHS and RHS.
            *   Start with the element having the most atoms (e.g., O in $Fe_3O_4$).
            *   Balance oxygen by adding coefficients to $H_2O$.
            *   Balance hydrogen.
            *   Balance iron.
            *   Final check of all elements.
        *   **(b)** Reiterate the Law of Conservation of Mass and how balancing ensures it.

2.  **(a) What are displacement reactions? Give one chemical equation representing a displacement reaction.**
    **(b) What are double displacement reactions? Give one chemical equation representing a double displacement reaction.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **(a)** Define displacement reaction (a more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound).
        *   Provide an example like $Zn + CuSO_4 \rightarrow ZnSO_4 + Cu$ or $Fe + CuSO_4 \rightarrow FeSO_4 + Cu$.
        *   **(b)** Define double displacement reaction (reactions where atoms or ions are exchanged between two compounds).
        *   Provide an example like $AgNO_3 + NaCl \rightarrow AgCl + NaNO_3$ (precipitation) or $BaCl_2 + Na_2SO_4 \rightarrow BaSO_4 + 2NaCl$.
        *   Mention that one of the products is often a precipitate or a gas.

3.  **(a) Explain the terms 'oxidised' and 'reduced' with respect to gain or loss of oxygen and hydrogen. Give an example.**
    **(b) Identify the substance oxidised and the substance reduced in the following reaction: $CuO + H_2 \rightarrow Cu + H_2O$.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **(a)**
            *   **Oxidation:** Gain of oxygen OR loss of hydrogen.
            *   **Reduction:** Loss of oxygen OR gain of hydrogen.
            *   Give an example: $2Cu + O_2 \rightarrow 2CuO$ (Cu is oxidised). $H_2S + Cl_2 \rightarrow S + 2HCl$ ($H_2S$ is oxidised).
        *   **(b)**
            *   Identify that $CuO$ loses oxygen to become $Cu$, so $CuO$ is reduced.
            *   Identify that $H_2$ gains oxygen to become $H_2O$, so $H_2$ is oxidised.
            *   Write the balanced equation.

4.  **What is an exothermic reaction? Give an example of an exothermic reaction and write its balanced chemical equation. Explain why it is exothermic.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   Define exothermic reaction (a reaction that releases energy, usually in the form of heat).
        *   Give an example: Combustion of methane ($CH_4$).
        *   Write the balanced equation: $CH_4(g) + 2O_2(g) \rightarrow CO_2(g) + 2H_2O(l) + Energy$.
        *   Explain that the bonds formed in the products store less energy than the bonds broken in the reactants, leading to the release of excess energy.

5.  **When a solution of silver nitrate is added to a solution of sodium chloride, a white precipitate is formed, and the colour of the solution changes.
    (a) Identify the type of reaction that takes place.
    (b) Write the balanced chemical equation for this reaction.
    (c) Name the white precipitate formed.
    (d) Write the word equation for the reaction.**
    *   **Outline Answer:**
        *   **(a)** Double displacement reaction (precipitation reaction).
        *   **(b)** $AgNO_3(aq) + NaCl(aq) \rightarrow AgCl(s) + NaNO_3(aq)$
        *   **(c)** Silver chloride ($AgCl$).
        *   **(d)** Silver nitrate + Sodium chloride → Silver chloride + Sodium nitrate.
