## Chemical Reactions and Equations: Summary

This chapter introduces the fundamental concepts of chemical reactions and their representations. A chemical reaction involves a change in the nature and identity of substances, indicated by observations like changes in state, colour, gas evolution, or temperature.

We represent these reactions using word equations, listing reactants on the left and products on the right, separated by an arrow. A more concise method is using chemical equations, which employ chemical formulae of the reactants and products.

Crucially, chemical equations must be balanced to adhere to the **law of conservation of mass**, meaning the number of atoms of each element must be equal on both sides of the equation. Unbalanced equations are called skeletal equations. Balancing ensures that mass is neither lost nor gained during a chemical reaction. The chapter lays the groundwork for understanding various types of chemical reactions.
