## NCERT Class 10 Science: How do Organisms Reproduce?

### Section 1: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

**Instructions:** Choose the correct option. Each question carries 1 mark.

1.  Reproduction is necessary for the survival of:
    a) An individual organism
    b) A species
    c) A cell
    d) A protein

2.  The blueprint for body design in organisms is found in:
    a) Proteins
    b) DNA
    c) Carbohydrates
    d) Water

3.  When a cell divides to create new individuals, this process is called:
    a) Respiration
    b) Excretion
    c) Fission
    d) Nutrition

4.  Amoeba reproduces by a process called:
    a) Budding
    b) Fragmentation
    c) Binary fission
    d) Multiple fission

5.  The malarial parasite, Plasmodium, divides into many daughter cells at once by:
    a) Binary fission
    b) Multiple fission
    c) Budding
    d) Fragmentation

6.  Yeast reproduces by a process where small buds grow and separate. This is called:
    a) Fission
    b) Budding
    c) Fragmentation
    d) Regeneration

7.  In which organism does binary fission take place in any plane?
    a) Yeast
    b) Bacteria
    c) Amoeba
    d) Plasmodium

8.  DNA contains information for inheritance from parents to the next generation in the form of:
    a) Proteins
    b) Genes
    c) Chromosomes
    d) Molecules

9.  Variations during reproduction are important for:
    a) The death of the individual
    b) The survival of the species
    c) Making organisms identical
    d) Wasting energy

10. A single-celled organism splits into two equal halves during cell division. This is a type of:
    a) Budding
    b) Fragmentation
    c) Binary fission
    d) Multiple fission
