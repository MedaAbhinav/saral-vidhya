## NCERT Class 10 Science: How do Organisms Reproduce? (CBSE Board Prep)

### 1. Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

1.  Which of the following is the fundamental event in reproduction?
    A. Growth of the organism
    B. Creation of a DNA copy
    C. Excretion of waste products
    D. Intake of food

    **Answer:** B. Creation of a DNA copy

2.  A variation in DNA copying is most likely to be beneficial for:
    A. The individual organism
    B. The survival of the species
    C. The stability of the ecosystem
    D. The process of photosynthesis

    **Answer:** B. The survival of the species

3.  Binary fission is a mode of asexual reproduction observed in:
    A. Yeast
    B. Amoeba
    C. Plasmodium
    D. Hydra

    **Answer:** B. Amoeba

4.  Multiple fission is a mode of reproduction where:
    A. A single cell divides into two daughter cells.
    B. A single cell divides into many daughter cells simultaneously.
    C. An outgrowth develops into a new individual.
    D. The organism breaks into fragments, each forming a new individual.

    **Answer:** B. A single cell divides into many daughter cells simultaneously.

5.  Which of the following organisms reproduces by budding?
    A. Amoeba
    B. Bacteria
    C. Yeast
    D. Plasmodium

    **Answer:** C. Yeast

6.  Fragmentation is a mode of reproduction in:
    A. Unicellular organisms
    B. Yeast
    C. Spirogyra
    D. Bacteria

    **Answer:** C. Spirogyra

7.  Regeneration is a characteristic feature of:
    A. Hydra
    B. Amoeba
    C. Yeast
    D. Bacteria

    **Answer:** A. Hydra

8.  Vegetative propagation is a type of asexual reproduction in plants where:
    A. Spores are produced.
    B. New plants arise from vegetative parts like roots, stems, and leaves.
    C. Pollen grains are involved.
    D. Seeds are formed.

    **Answer:** B. New plants arise from vegetative parts like roots, stems, and leaves.

9.  Which part of the plant is commonly used for vegetative propagation of potato?
    A. Root
    B. Leaf
    C. Bud (eyes)
    D. Flower

    **Answer:** C. Bud (eyes)

10. The fusion of male and female gametes is called:
    A. Vegetative propagation
    B. Fertilization
    C. Budding
    D. Fission

    **Answer:** B. Fertilization

### 2. Short Answer Questions (2-3 Marks)

1.  **What is the significance of DNA copying in reproduction?**
    *   **Model Answer:** DNA copying is the fundamental event in reproduction because it ensures that the genetic information or the blueprint for the body design is passed on from the parent organism to the offspring. This ensures that new individuals are created with characteristics similar to their parents.

2.  **Why is variation beneficial to the species but not necessarily for the individual?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Variation is beneficial to the species as it increases the chances of survival if the environment changes drastically. Some individuals with advantageous variations might survive and reproduce, ensuring the continuation of the species. For an individual, a variation might not always be beneficial and could even be harmful, leading to death.

3.  **Distinguish between binary fission and multiple fission.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Binary fission is a type of asexual reproduction where a single-celled organism divides into two approximately equal daughter cells. Multiple fission, on the other hand, involves a single-celled organism dividing into many daughter cells simultaneously, often occurring under unfavorable conditions.

4.  **Explain the process of budding in yeast.**
    *   **Model Answer:** In yeast, budding is a mode of asexual reproduction. A small outgrowth called a bud develops from the parent cell. This bud grows and eventually detaches from the parent cell to become a new, independent yeast organism.

5.  **What is fragmentation? Give an example of an organism that reproduces by fragmentation.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Fragmentation is a mode of asexual reproduction where an organism breaks down into several fragments, and each fragment grows into a new individual. Spirogyra, a filamentous alga, reproduces by fragmentation.

6.  **Explain regeneration with an example.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Regeneration is the ability of a cut or broken organism to grow back into a whole new organism. For example, if a Hydra is cut into several pieces, each piece can regenerate into a complete new Hydra.

7.  **What is vegetative propagation? Mention any two methods.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Vegetative propagation is a type of asexual reproduction in plants where new plants arise from vegetative parts like roots, stems, and leaves. Two methods are:
        1.  **Cuttings:** A piece of stem or root is cut and planted to grow into a new plant (e.g., rose, sugarcane).
        2.  **Grafting:** A part of one plant is joined to the rootstock of another plant to combine desirable traits (e.g., fruit trees).

8.  **How do plants like Bryophyllum reproduce vegetatively?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Bryophyllum reproduces vegetatively through its leaves. Small buds develop along the margins of its leaves. When these leaves fall on the soil, these buds grow into new plants.

9.  **What is the difference between asexual and sexual reproduction?**
    *   **Model Answer:** Asexual reproduction involves only one parent and produces offspring that are genetically identical to the parent. Sexual reproduction involves two parents (male and female), fusion of gametes, and produces offspring that are genetically different from the parents and each other.

10. **Define fertilization.**
    *   **Model Answer:** Fertilization is the process of fusion of male and female gametes (sperms and eggs) to form a diploid zygote. This is a crucial step in sexual reproduction.

### 4. Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS) Questions

1.  If a population of bacteria is subjected to a sudden drastic increase in temperature, what outcome would you expect if they were reproducing asexually, and what outcome would you expect if they were reproducing sexually? Justify your answer in terms of variation.
2.  Consider a scenario where a species is very successful and occupies a stable niche. How might the process of reproduction (asexual vs. sexual) influence the long-term adaptability of this species if the niche were to suddenly change?
3.  During DNA replication, a mistake occurs that results in a protein that is less efficient but not entirely non-functional. How could this specific type of variation eventually lead to a new trait that might be advantageous for the organism in a specific environment? Explain the role of time and natural selection.
