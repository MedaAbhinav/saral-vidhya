In "How do Organisms Reproduce?", the fundamental concept is that reproduction ensures the continuation of species, not individual survival. Organisms reproduce by creating copies of their body designs, which are dictated by DNA. DNA copying is a crucial step, but it's not always perfect. Chemical reactions involved in copying DNA can lead to minor variations. While drastic variations can be lethal, subtle variations are vital for the survival of a species. If an environment changes, individuals with advantageous variations are more likely to survive and reproduce, ensuring the species' persistence. These variations are the raw material for evolution.
